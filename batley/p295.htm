Elizabeth Davis

#8190, b. 1871
Elizabeth Davis|b. 1871|p295.htm#i8190|James Davis||p295.htm#i8186|Jane (?)|b. c 1832|p294.htm#i8184|||||||||||||
     Elizabeth Davis was born in 1871 at Hanbury, Worcestershire. She was the daughter of James Davis and Jane (?). She was listed in the census of 5 April 1891 as a servant in the household of George Sheard at The Woodlands, Batley.1
Elizabeth Davis was gardener James's younger sister, and may have been recommended by him; now employ a cook and two other domestic servants. She married William T Webber in 1900 back in her native Worcestershire.2

Emma Day

#8200, b. circa 1863
     Emma Day was born circa 1863 at North Hardwick, Worksop,, daughter of William Day, a gamekeeper. She was listed in the census of 3 April 1881 as a servant in the household of George Sheard at Upper Batley Lane, Batley.1
Emma Day came from the same village as her older co-worker, Mary Salman. It is likely that both their fathers, a gamekeeper and forester respectively, worked for the Clumber Park Estate; There are now three new domestic staff at the Woodlands.

Frederick Barker

#8201, b. circa 1833
Frederick Barker|b. c 1833|p295.htm#i8201||||Sarah Barker||p295.htm#i8202|||||||||||||
     Frederick Barker was born circa 1833 at Baslow, Derbyshire. He was the son of Sarah Barker. He was listed in the census of 30 March 1851 at Bubnell as a groom, gardener, etc living with his widowed mother and his brothers.1
Frederick Barker married Agnes (?).
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Cool Hill, Warley, as a gardener.2
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Upper Batley Lane, Batley, as Gardener.3
Shown living adjacent to George Sheard and family, Frederick Barker was presumably by now employed by him and living in the lodge of The Woodlands.
By the census of 5 April 1891 Frederick and Agnes were living at Gardener's Lodge, Hexgreave Park, Farnsfield, Nottinghamshire, where Frederick was gardener for the larger estate of Hexgreave Hall.4
