Jonas Oddy

#5261

Relationship=7th great-grandfather of Vivien Eley.
Charts
Ancestry of Vivien Eley Tomlinson
     Jonas Oddy married Grace Rodwell on 24 June 1719 at Batley.1
Jonas Oddy made a will on 23 October 1771 at Dale House, Heckmondwike. Describing himself as a clothier, he makes his eldest son George Oddy and Joseph Allott his son-in-law his executors, It is to them that he leaves his personal estate, goods and chattels, and he wishes them to occupy his house, passing to them under tenant right as his sons subject to the acceptance of his landlord, a Mr Furnisk (probably Furness) His goods are to be valued and for every pound in value of the valuation they are to pay his wife a shilling a year, to be paid quarterly.
To younger sons John and William he leaves a shilling apiece, and a shilling also to his daughter Mary her heirs and assigns (written as ares and asines, the spelling is rather phonetic!)

witnesses were Jeremiah Firth and Thomas Lister.2

George Oddy

#5268, b. 1735
George Oddy|b. 1735|p182.htm#i5268|Jonas Oddy||p182.htm#i5261|Grace Rodwell||p182.htm#i5262|||||||||||||

Relationship=7th great-uncle of Vivien Eley.
     George Oddy was born in 1735. He was the son of Jonas Oddy and Grace Rodwell. George Oddy was baptized on 18 February 1734/35 at Batley.1
This George Oddy is something as a puzzle as the entry is clear in Batley records, and I had thought that the elder George must have died, in which case it would have been quite usual to give another son the same name. however, the George mentioned in his father's will is the eldest son, still alive so not this George. So maybe he was son of another Jonas, or another Oddy.

James Thurmand

#5273, b. 11 September 1803, d. 6 August 1895
James Thurmand|b. 11 Sep 1803\nd. 6 Aug 1895|p182.htm#i5273|William Thurman|b. 1777|p42.htm#i1234|Elizabeth Firth||p182.htm#i5271|James Thurman|b. 1731|p41.htm#i1226|Ann Fearnsides|b. 1738|p41.htm#i1227|John Firth||p182.htm#i5275||||

Relationship=1st cousin 5 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     James Thurmand was born on 11 September 1803. He was the son of William Thurman and Elizabeth Firth. James Thurmand was baptized on 18 September 1803 at Batley.1
He married Sarah Day, daughter of Jonathan Day and Rebecca Senior, on 13 March 1834 at Dewsbury.2
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Batley Carr, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer. Also present is Simon Day, 15, an apprentice.3
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Batley Carr, Batley, as a master manufacturer, woollen employing 12 men and 5 women and one of a firm of employing 41 men, 30 women and 40 children.4
On 23 May 1877 James Thurmand and his sons, who had been trading as James Thurmand and Sons, woollen manufacturers, of Batley Carr, filed a petition f0r bankruptcy.5
He died on 6 August 1895 at age 91.6
