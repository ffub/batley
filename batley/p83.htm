Arthur Walter Hanstock

#2510, b. 1875, d. 1949

Charts
Descendants of William Blackburn
     Arthur Walter Hanstock was born in 1875 at Batley. He was the son of Walter Hanstock and Sarah Ann Blackburn. Arthur Walter Hanstock was baptized on 30 May 1878 at St. Thomas's, Batley.
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the son of Walter Hanstock at Lamplands, Field Hill, Batley.1,2
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as the son of Walter Hanstock at 35 Bath Street, North Meols, Southport, Lancashire.3
In 1898 Arthur Walter Hanstock who had been articled to the firm was made a partner in his father Walter's architectural practice.
Arthur Walter Hanstock married Alice Maud Jubb, daughter of John Joseph Jubb and Matilda Sheard, on 14 July 1898.4
Arthur Walter Hanstock took over the practice on the death of his father Walter in 1900.
Arthur Walter Hanstock lived at The Gables, Upper Batley. He was listed as the head of household in the 31 March 1901 census at York Road, Batley, as an architect and surveyor.5

Batley Town Hall
Although his father had worked on designs for a new town hall, when the old one was badly damaged by fire in 1902 it was decided , in view of other heavy expenditure anticipated , that this was too expensive a scheme, and it fell to Arthur Walter to re-build and extend the old one, now with a Council Chamber and Mayor's Parlour, and entrance, not from Commercial Street as before but from the Market Place.

In the census of 2 April 1911 he was listed as the head of household at The Gables, Upper Batley, Batley, as an architect and land agent to Lord Wilton.6

Arthur Walter Hanstock was associated with his father in the design and erection of the Leeds City meat market extension, Leeds City Public Baths, Selby Public Baths, Ossett Town Hall and many other buildings of note.

He took over the Batley practice on his father's death, and among the many buildings in the locality for which he was the architect are:-
Batley Library, 1906.
the new Blakeridge Mills of Messrs. J. T. and J. Taylor, Ltd.
The Lady Ann Mills of Messrs. J.B. and W. Hirst Ltd.
a new mill for The Coloured Cotton Spinning Company at Mirfield,
Healey School, Batley,
extensions to the Batley Technical College,
several cinemas in the district.

He was a land agent to the Earl of Wilton, and to the Wilton Estate, Batley, an architect to the old Dewsbury Board of Guardians and to the Batley Education Committee, and honorary architect to the Batley District Hospital.
He died in 1949 at Scarborough.7

Children of Arthur Walter Hanstock and Alice Maud Jubb

  • John Walter Hanstock b. 1899, d. 30 Oct 1918
  • Doris Hanstock b. 1905
  • Arthur Gilbert Hanstock b. 1910, d. c 2006
