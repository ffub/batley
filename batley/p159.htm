Abraham Burnley1

#4665, b. 24 January 1812
Abraham Burnley|b. 24 Jan 1812|p159.htm#i4665|John Burnley|b. 14 Jul 1779\nd. 2 Jan 1836|p12.htm#i365|Elizabeth Blakeley|b. 5 Mar 1780\nd. 1842|p12.htm#i366|Michael Burnley|b. 1748|p11.htm#i353|Ruth Blakeley|b. 1752|p11.htm#i352|John Blakeley|b. 1739|p6.htm#i182|Sarah Swallow|b. 1740|p13.htm#i382|

Relationship=2nd cousin 5 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     Abraham Burnley was born on 24 January 1812.1 He was the son of John Burnley and Elizabeth Blakeley.1 Abraham Burnley was baptized on 4 February 1812 at Salem Chapel, Dewsbury.1
He married Sarah Jackson, daughter of Benjamin Jackson, on 11 July 1838 at Bramham Assumed to be the right marriage in that Abraham married a Sarah, birthplace Leeds, and some of children have middle name Jackson.2,3
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Havercroft, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer.4
1844 saw Abraham Burnley and George Sheard representing Batley interests on the provisional Committee of the proposed Leeds, Dewsbury and Manchester Junction Railway, chaired by Leeds manufacturer John Gott.5
In 1845 Abraham Burnley was pulled out of a partnership with his brothers Samuel and Jacob.6
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household as a wool stapler.7
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Albert Street, Batley, as a woollen and rag merchant, a widower.8

  • Mary E Burnley b. 1841

#4677, b. 15 November 1829, d. 18 August 1895
Abraham Gledhill|b. 15 Nov 1829\nd. 18 Aug 1895|p159.htm#i4677|Joseph Gledhill|b. 1 Mar 1787\nd. 17 Jun 1858|p72.htm#i2166|Sarah Briggs|b. 1788\nd. 29 May 1861|p64.htm#i1941|John Gledhill|b. 1767\nd. 12 Feb 1844|p44.htm#i1292|Susanna Newsome|b. 1766\nd. 3 Dec 1846|p44.htm#i1293|James Briggs|b. 1763|p64.htm#i1940|Dinah Sheard|b. 28 Apr 1765\nd. 17 Mar 1842|p4.htm#i104|

Charts
Descendants of Michael Sheard born 1688
     Abraham Gledhill was born on 15 November 1829.1 He was the son of Joseph Gledhill and Sarah Briggs. In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the son of Joseph Gledhill at Havercroft Lane End, Batley.2
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as a cloth weaver the son of Joseph Gledhill at Havercroft, Batley.3
Abraham Gledhill married Matilda Hepworth, daughter of Sarah Hepworth, on 16 May 1853 (I would thank a descendant of Abraham and Matilda for information on this family).1
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Howley Park, Morley, as a woollen weaver.4

This was the Park of Howley Hall, once home of the Saviles, but blown up around 1730.
Whilst such ruins as were left above ground gave little indication of the house's former grandeur, cellars remained, and in doing a little towards clearing these, Abraham Gledhill found a quantity of animal bones locally supposed to be the remains of a large bear said to have been kept at the Hall, and also, and this is still in the possession of a descendant, a silver coin bearing the inscription "Guglielmus III.,1697."1,5

In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Howley Hill, Morley, as a tenterer at mill.6
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Bradford Road, Batley, as a cloth tenterer.7
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as the head of household as a cloth tenterer.8
He died on 18 August 1895 at age 65; of acute meningitis.

     Thomas Gledhill was born on 2 March 1806.1 He was the son of John Gledhill and Susanna Newsome.1 Thomas Gledhill was baptized on 20 April 1806 at Batley.2
He married Ellen Keighley, daughter of Matthew Keighley and Grace (?), on 16 August 1824 at Batley.2
Thomas Gledhill was Thomas is thought to have been a shareholder in Clerk Green Mill, and in 1841 was listed as in partnership there with Mark Terry, Robert Brearley the elder and sons Robert and Daniel, Henry Hall and James Redfearn as Brearley Terry and Company, scribblers and fullers.3
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at New Batley, Batley, as a manufacturer.4
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at New Street, Batley, as a woollen cloth weaver.5
