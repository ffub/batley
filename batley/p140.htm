     Henry Dixon Smith was born on 7 September 1847 at Batley.1 He was the son of William Smith and Mary Chew. Henry Dixon Smith was baptized on 24 October 1847 at Batley.1
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the son of William Smith at Killin, Batley.2
Henry Dixon Smith married Eliza Holliday in 1870.3
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Dark Lane, Batley, as a warehouseman.4
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Broomfield House, Bradford Road, Batley, as a commercial clerk in a mungo and shoddy business.5
An H. D. Smith was one of the "oldest employees" employees of John Blackburn, Old Mill who was present at the dinner held in 1910 to celebrate carrier Jack Wood's long service with the firm, indeed participating in songs and duets sung on that occasion. At this time he was living adjacently to Jack in Broomfield House, and I think it likely that he was already working at the Old Mill.
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as the head of household at 21 Bradford Road, Batley, as a clerk, bookkeeper, a widower.6
In the census of 31 March 1901 he was listed as the head of household at 7 Bath Street, Batley, as a commercial clerk, a widower.7
Henry Dixon Smith died in 1922.

Children of Henry Dixon Smith and Eliza Holliday

  • Clara Ann Smith b. 1871
  • Alice M Smith b. c 1874

     Ann Shackleton was born in 1832 at Holbeck, Leeds. She was the daughter of Richard Shackleton and Ann (?). In the census of 6 June 1841 she was listed as aged 8 the daughter of Richard Shackleton at Providence House, Drighlington, Birstall.1
In the census of 30 March 1851 she was listed as aged 18 the sister of Thomas Walker Shackleton.2

Caleb Burnley

#4185, b. 1832
Caleb Burnley|b. 1832|p140.htm#i4185|John Burnley|b. 2 Jun 1805|p141.htm#i4189|Harriet Fox|b. c 1805\nd. 8 Jul 1851|p141.htm#i4190|John Burnley|b. 14 Jul 1779\nd. 2 Jan 1836|p12.htm#i365|Elizabeth Blakeley|b. 5 Mar 1780\nd. 1842|p12.htm#i366|Phineas Fox||p189.htm#i5458|Elizabeth (?)|d. 9 Oct 1849|p189.htm#i5459|

Relationship=3rd cousin 4 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     Caleb Burnley was born in 1832 at Batley. He was the son of John Burnley and Harriet Fox. Caleb Burnley was baptized on 4 March 1832 at Batley.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the son of John Burnley at Havercroft, Batley.2
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as a manufacturer's son the son of John Burnley at Havercroft, Batley.3
Caleb Burnley married Letitia Fox, daughter of David Fox and Rachel Jubb, on 16 September 1852 at Batley. A further indication that this is the correct marriage is that Letitia's brother Joseph Jubb Fox is with Caleb and Letitia in the 1861 census.4,5
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Branch Road, Batley, as a bookkeeper in a woollen mill, aged 29.6
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Wellington Street, Batley, as a tenterer.7

Robert Shackleton

#4186, b. circa 1818, d. 24 September 1874
Robert Shackleton|b. c 1818\nd. 24 Sep 1874|p140.htm#i4186|Richard Shackleton||p140.htm#i4180|Ann (?)||p140.htm#i4181|||||||||||||
     Robert Shackleton was born circa 1818 at Leeds. He was the son of Richard Shackleton and Ann (?). In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as a grocer and watchmaker aged 33 the brother of Thomas Walker Shackleton.1 Titus Sheard treasured a bible given to him by Robert Shackleton when Titus was a pupil at Zion Sunday School.2
He married Rachel Jubb, daughter of Joseph Jubb and Letitia Talbot, and widow of David Fox, in 1855.3 In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Up Lane, Batley, as a rag merchant employing 5 women.4
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Hanover Street, Batley, as Borough Accountant (Batley's first) Two of his wife's grandsons, David and John Joseph Burnley, were with him at this time. The former was described as junior clerk to accountant and I imagine was assisting Robert. John Joseph was described as junior clerk to the Gas Works office.5
Robert Shackleton died on 24 September 1874 at Batley.6
He was succeeded as Borough Accountant by Isaac Binns.6
