Nathan Bolton

#8062, b. circa 1830
Nathan Bolton|b. c 1830|p290.htm#i8062||||Elizabeth Bolton|b. 5 Sep 1802|p289.htm#i8047|||||||Charles Bolton|b. c 1772\nd. 18 Sep 1839|p289.htm#i8041|Sarah Saville|b. 18 Mar 1775\nd. 23 May 1838|p289.htm#i8042|
     Nathan Bolton was born circa 1830. He was the son of Elizabeth Bolton. Nathan Bolton was baptized on 16 January 1831 at Batley as son of Elizabeth Bolton, singlewoman.1
He appeared on the census of 6 June 1841 in the household of Richard Bolton at Howley, Morley.2 In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as a woollen cloth weaver, the son of Elizabeth Bolton at Howley, Morley.3
Nathan Bolton married Hannah Roberts in 1860.4
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household, at Howley, Morley, a dyer.5

George Brooke

#8084, b. 1823, d. 18 July 1889
George Brooke|b. 1823\nd. 18 Jul 1889|p290.htm#i8084|John Brooke||p255.htm#i7182|Elizabeth (?)||p290.htm#i8082|||||||||||||
     George Brooke was born in 1823. He was the son of John Brooke and Elizabeth (?). George Brooke was baptized on 20 August 1823 at Dewsbury.1
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as a wool agent the son of John Brooke at Rouse Mill, Soothill.2
George Brooke married Ellen Mallinson, daughter of James Mallinson and Mary (?), in 1859.3
George Brooke died on 18 July 1889 at Woodland House, Soothill, At the time of his death he was described as senior partner in the firm of Abraham Brooke & Co, wool merchants, established by his late brother.4 He was buried on 22 July 1889 at St Paul's, Hanging Heaton.
