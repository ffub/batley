Eli Ibberson

#7063, b. 16 January 1821
Eli Ibberson|b. 16 Jan 1821|p251.htm#i7063|Abraham Ibberson|b. c 1768\nd. 4 Apr 1848|p14.htm#i431|Mary Richardson||p35.htm#i1045|William Ibberson|b. c 1742\nd. 2 Jan 1837|p33.htm#i997|Sarah Senior||p33.htm#i998|||||||
     Eli Ibberson was born on 16 January 1821. He was the son of Abraham Ibberson and Mary Richardson. Eli Ibberson was baptized on 23 April 1821 at Old Chapel Independent, Heckmondwike.
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the son of Abraham Ibberson at Havercroft, Batley.1
Eli Ibberson married Elizabeth Hooley in 1849.2
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Havercroft, Batley, as a farmer of 30 acres employing 2 labourers. There is a house eervant, Ellen Pashley, and two farm labourers, George Jackson and William Stringer. His wife is listed as a draper.3
The drapery business seems to have becomne a family concern, and it is likely that, with an expanding business, Eli moved into the buildings formerly the residence of Michael Sheard when the latter moved to Dryfield House around 1857.4
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Commercial Street, Batley, as a draper. The drapery business ten years earlier described as the venture of Eli's wife has apparently expanded, and, reflecting the demand from the ladies of those families benefitting from Batley's increased prosperity, two milliners, Mary Lumbsden and Sarah Senior, are employed.
a house servant/nurse Olive Station(?, and two apprentices, Joseph Demeline and Greenwood Blakeley.5
In 1869 Eli Ibberson was was elected one of Batley's first Town Councillors.
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Commercial Street, Batley, a Draper and Town Councillor. Blakeley Greenwood is employed as an assistant, and there are an apprentice, two milliners and a general servant.6
He was responsible for the arangements for his former neighbour and fellow councillor John Sheard's funeral on 16 September 1875 at Batley Cemetery
; The funeral arrangements were said to have been admirably carried out by .7,8 In 1881 Eli Ibberson had retired and was living in Ilkley.

Children of Eli Ibberson and Elizabeth Hooley

  • Mary E Ibberson b. 1853
  • John George Ibberson b. 1860
