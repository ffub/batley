Owen Willans Richardson

#5518, b. 26 April 1879, d. 15 February 1959
Owen Willans Richardson|b. 26 Apr 1879\nd. 15 Feb 1959|p192.htm#i5518|Joshua Henry Richardson||p192.htm#i5517|Charlotte Maria Willans|b. c 1852|p191.htm#i5516|||||||James Willans|b. 1817|p190.htm#i5479|Mary F. Dixon||p190.htm#i5482|

Relationship=6th cousin 2 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     Owen Willans Richardson was born on 26 April 1879 at Dewsbury. He was the son of Joshua Henry Richardson and Charlotte Maria Willans. Owen Willans Richardson was educated at Batley Grammar School. In 1928 Owen Willans Richardson was awarded a Nobel Prize for Physics.
He received a knighthood in 1939. He died on 15 February 1959 at age 79.

Selina Cawthorn

#5536, b. circa 1819
Selina Cawthorn|b. c 1819|p192.htm#i5536|James Cawthorn||p286.htm#i7965||||||||||||||||
     Selina Cawthorn was born circa 1819 at Lee Fair. She was the daughter of James Cawthorn.
Selina Cawthorn married Christopher Burnley, son of John Burnley and Ann Stubley, on 7 July 1844 at Batley.1
In the census of 30 March 1851 she was listed as in the household of Christopher Burnley at New Street, Batley.2
In the census of 7 April 1861 she was listed as the head of household at Cobden Street, Batley, as a rag sorter.3
Selina Burnley married Joshua Stubley, son of Rachel Stubley, on 29 September 1862 at Dewsbury described as widower and widow. Joshua was Selina's first husband's cousin.4
In the census of 2 April 1871 she was listed as the head of household at Peel St, Batley, aged 52 and now widowed for the second time.5
In the census of 3 April 1881 she was listed as the head of household at Brearley St, Batley, as a widow.6
She was listed in the census of 5 April 1891 at Quarry St, Soothill, as a widow.7

Children of Selina Cawthorn and Christopher Burnley

  • John Cawthorne Burnley b. 2 Oct 1844
  • Hephzebah Burnley+ b. 1849
  • Arthur Burnley b. 6 Jun 1851

     Hephzebah Burnley was born in 1849 at Batley.1 She was the daughter of Christopher Burnley and Selina Cawthorn.
In the census of 30 March 1851 she was listed as the daughter of Christopher Burnley at New Street, Batley.2
As of 1851, Hephzebah Burnley was also known as Elizabeth Burnley.
In the census of 7 April 1861 she was listed as a scholar the daughter of Selina Burnley at Cobden Street, Batley.3
Hephzebah Burnley married Joseph Whiteley on 19 September 1870 at All Saints, Batley, witnesses were John Colbeck and Joseph Chadwick.4
Hephzebah Whiteley appeared on the census of 2 April 1871 in the household of her mother Selina Stubley at Peel St, Batley; aged 52 and now widowed for the second time.5 As of 1881, Hephzebah Burnley was also known as Elizabeth Whiteley.
In the census of 3 April 1881 she was listed as formerly weaver,, the daughter of Selina Stubley at Brearley St, Batley.6

Children of Hephzebah Burnley and Joseph Whiteley
