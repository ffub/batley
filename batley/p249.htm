Abraham Bruce

#7009, b. 1829
Abraham Bruce|b. 1829|p249.htm#i7009|Abraham Bruce||p187.htm#i5386|Sarah Kitson|d. 1840|p136.htm#i4076|||||||John Kitson||p186.htm#i5384||||
      Abraham Bruce was also known as Abraham Robinson In the 1851 census and subsequently Abraham uses his stepfather's name of Robinson. He was born in 1829. He was the son of Abraham Bruce and Sarah Kitson. Abraham Bruce was baptized on 8 June 1829 at Birstall.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as in the household of John Robinson at New Batley, Batley.2
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as a bobbin winder, the son-in-law, though actually the step-son, of John Robinson.3 He was listed in the census of 7 April 1861.
Abraham Robinson appeared as a lodger in the census of 7 April 1861 in the household of Sam Pearson and Sarah Elizabeth Ellen Pearson, his half sister and her husband at Hume Street, Batley.4 In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as as a labourer, and under the name of Abraham Bruce, the brother-in-law of Sam Pearson at High St, Batley.5
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as a pea dealer ( this has been altered to tea) the brother-in-law of Sam Pearson at High St, Batley.6
He was listed a pea hawker, described as uncle, in the census of 5 April 1891in the household of Sam Pearson at Park Rd, Batley, as a widower, an engine fitter.7 In the census of 31 March 1901 he was listed as the brother-in-law of Sam Pearson at 8 Granville Place, Batley.8

     James Alexander Parr was born circa 1844 at Leith, Midlothian, Scotland. He was the son of George Parr and Martha Dixon.1 In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as aged 17, a stationer, the son of Martha Parr at 4 Furnace St, Glasgow, Lanarkshire, Scotland.2
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as as a woollen manufacturer the son of Martha Parr at Stanley Terrrace, Batley.3
In 1878 James Alexander Parr was in view of a later reference likely to have been the Parr in the firm of Parr and Ramsden listed as woollen manufacturers, Bradford Road, Batley.4
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Bleak House, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer, employs about 120 hands. there is a housekeeper, Hannah Taylor.5
In 1884 a partnership with Joe Ramsden, trading as Parr and Ramsden at Brookroyd Mills, was dissolved, the business to carry on by Mr Ramsden on his own account trading as Joe Ramsden.6
