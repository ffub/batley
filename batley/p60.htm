Mark Pyrah

#1770, b. 1781, d. 23 March 1852
Mark Pyrah|b. 1781\nd. 23 Mar 1852|p60.htm#i1770|Joseph Pyrah|b. 1756\nd. 25 Apr 1806|p48.htm#i1429|Elizabeth Gledhill|b. 1760\nd. 5 Oct 1825|p57.htm#i1669|Joshua Pyrah|d. 1796|p21.htm#i629|Sarah Beaumont|d. 1786|p21.htm#i630|Joseph Gledhill||p57.htm#i1670|Grace Wood||p77.htm#i2302|

Relationship=1st cousin 6 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     Mark Pyrah age from tombstone. He was born in 1781 at Soothill. He was the son of Joseph Pyrah and Elizabeth Gledhill. Mark Pyrah was baptized on 4 June 1781 at Dewsbury.
He married Elizabeth Hardcastle, daughter of John Hardcastle and Jane Preston, on 25 July 1799 at Batley witnesses Wm Gledhill and Jeremiah Pyrah.1
Mark Pyrah witnessed the marriage of Mary Thompson and Jeremiah Pyrah on 11 April 1803 at Batley.1 In 1813 Mark Pyrah was probably the Mark Pyrah, described as a blanket manufacturer of Clerk Green, who spent some time in Rothwell Gaol as an insolvent debtor. Ther was some dispute about his share in a mill at Clerk Green.2
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Clerk Green, Batley, as a woollen lab.3
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Clerk Green, Batley, as a widower aged 69, late overlooker, woollen mill.4
He died on 23 March 1852 at Batley. He was buried on 25 March 1852 at Batley ; aged 70, of Clerk Green.1
