Jeremiah Hudswell

#4331, b. 28 October 1794, d. 3 August 1852
Jeremiah Hudswell|b. 28 Oct 1794\nd. 3 Aug 1852|p146.htm#i4331|Thomas Hudswell|b. c 1770\nd. 7 May 1832|p24.htm#i715|Elizabeth Hall|b. 1774\nd. 24 Apr 1842|p24.htm#i712||||Grace Hudswell|b. 7 Feb 1770|p262.htm#i7363|Joseph Hall|b. 1746\nd. Oct 1808|p23.htm#i694|Ann Turner|b. 1746|p12.htm#i363|

Relationship=2nd cousin 5 times removed of Vivien Eley.
Charts
Descendants of Joseph Hall
     Jeremiah Hudswell was born on 28 October 1794 at Batley. He was the son of Thomas Hudswell and Elizabeth Hall. Jeremiah Hudswell was baptized on 16 November 1794 at Batley.1
He married Elizabeth Pyrah, daughter of Benjamin Pyrah and Sally Firth, on 19 January 1817 at Batley Both wrote their names clearly. The marriage was witnessed by William Parr and Thomas Lee.1
In 1829 Jeremiah Hudswell was listed in a Dewsbury directory as a horse clothing manufacturer.2
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Up Lane, Batley, as a clothier.3
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Up Lane, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer. A grandson, Edwin aged 2 is also present.4
He died on 3 August 1852 aged 57 years.5
Jeremiah Hudswell was probably the Jerre Hudswell to whom Samuel Colbeck refers as "saving Batley" during the hard times of the late 1830s. , saying:-
"Batley was in a very bad state. Cotton had fallen and shoddy had made very little progress. A man called Jerre Hudswell secured some government contracts for coverlets, and that saved Batley. We got into coverlet weaving. Jerre Hudswell found work for a great number of people. I applied for a job and got one, taking my own loom with me."6
