     Edgar Dean Hall was born in 1889 at Batley.1 He was the son of Louis Hall and Amelia Dean.1
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as aged 2 the son of Louis Hall at Ivy Cottage, 25 Purlwell Lane, Batley.2
In the census of 31 March 1901 he was listed as the son of Louis Hall at High St., Uppingham, Rutland.3

Edgar Dean Hall had a distinguished wartime career, first gaining a commission in the Northumberland Fusiliers then transferring to the Royal Flying Corps. He was a member of 101 Squadron. Formed at Farnborough on 12th July 1917, it moved to the Western Front, flying FE2 biplanes against enemy targets in France and Belgium.4
In 1918 he was awarded the Croix de Guerre.4,5
In WWII he is understood to have taken an emergency commission in the REME, retiring as a Major.4
