Benjamin Senior

#2718, b. 13 January 1800, d. 10 September 1872
Benjamin Senior|b. 13 Jan 1800\nd. 10 Sep 1872|p90.htm#i2718|John Senior|b. 21 May 1778|p110.htm#i3262|Elizabeth Colbeck|b. 1775|p89.htm#i2711|William Senior|b. 11 Jan 1756|p109.htm#i3257|Hannah Walker||p110.htm#i3259|Samuel Colbeck|b. 1739|p13.htm#i387|Nancy Booth|b. 1742|p13.htm#i388|
     Benjamin Senior was born on 13 January 1800 at Batley.1 He was the son of John Senior and Elizabeth Colbeck.
Benjamin Senior was baptized on 2 February 1800 at Batley.
He married Elizabeth Talbot, daughter of James Talbot and Mary Sunderland, on 5 July 1821 at Batley.2
In 1824 Benjamin Senior joined the congregation of the Methodist New Connexion, then housed in a Chapel at the top of Spa Hill.3
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Havercroft Lane end, Batley, as a rag dealer. George and Benjamin were partners in Sheard & Senior. A Sarah Talbot aged 10 is listed as a female servant.4
George Eayrs tells us that in his home Benjamin Senior and his wife cherished a little orphan girl who became Mrs. A. Ackroyd. This was  Sarah daughter of his wife's cousin James Talbot.3
In 1844 Benjamin Senior built a rag warehouse in Commercial Street.5
In 1845 Benjamin Senior was an executor for the Will of William Colbeck.6

In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Havercroft, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer firm of two employing 74 men and 22 women.7
A)This can I believe be identified as the partnership Sheard and Senior
Benjamin Senior and George Sheard were were living close to each other at the top of Hick Lane. Both mention a firm of 74 men, and in George's case 21 women, in Benjamin's case 22 women.
The partnership of George and Benjamin as Rag Merchants and woollen Manufacturers was dissolved by mutual consent on the 24th of March1854.7,8 A later article concerning Benjamin says:-
"Without going back to what seems ancient history in the staple trade of Batley, the late Mr. Senior was one of the earliest and most prosperous pioneers, and along with the late Mr John Jubb and Messrs Robert and George Blakeley of Dewsbury so succeeded in planting shoddy in this district of heavy woollen manufactory that now, whatever cloth is made, it is almost a "sine qua non" that shoddy is one of its component parts".

In 1855 Benjamin Senior was left £150 pounds in the will of George Sheard, his former business partner and one of his executors, as a token of a long friendship.
In 1856 Benjamin Senior lived at Hick Lane House, Batley.
He was listed as head of household in the 1861 census still at at Hick Lane, Batley.Now listed as a mungo merchant. A nurse and female servant are employed.9

Foundation of Zion Chapel
in 1864 Benjamin Senior urged the Methodist Congregation, who had built a new chapel at the junction of Commercial Street and Branch Road, to enlarge the chapel. It should be remembered that Batley's population was increasing fast, and it was decided to take down the existing structure and build one twice its size.3 Building material from the old chapel was used, but it was at the suggestion of Benjamin Sheard Benjamin's son-in-law, that entirely new material was provided for the facade of the present chapel, funded by Benjamin Sheard and Benjamin and his family.3
He lived in 1872 at Linefield House, Batley.10 He died on 10 September 1872 at age 72.10,1

Children of Benjamin Senior and Elizabeth Talbot

  • Benjamin Colbeck Senior b. 1833, d. 27 Mar 1837
  • Samuel Senior+ b. 1835, d. 4 Apr 1885
  • Mary Senior+ b. 2 Aug 1837, d. 4 Oct 1877
