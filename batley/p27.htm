John Fox

#813, b. 15 March 1786
John Fox|b. 15 Mar 1786|p27.htm#i813|Phineas Fox|b. 1755\nd. 14 Jul 1845|p7.htm#i186|Jane Blakeley|b. 1747|p7.htm#i184|Phineas Fox||p88.htm#i2665||||Benjamin Blakeley|b. 1712\nd. 1780|p6.htm#i178|Sarah Johnson|d. 1775|p6.htm#i179|

Relationship=1st cousin 6 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     John Fox was born on 15 March 1786.
He was the son of Phineas Fox and Jane Blakeley. John Fox was baptized on 2 April 1786 at Batley.1
He married Martha Richardson on 26 December 1806 at Batley.1
In 1815 Joseph Parker was apprenticed to John Fox as a boy to learn the manufacturing business. An account given by Edwin Law describes this apprenticeship as to learn the shoddy trade.2
In 1818John Fox is said to have united with Joseph Jubb and Phineas Fox, John Burnley and George Newsome to construct a rag machine.3
In 1823 John Fox was was one of those mentioned in the fourth part of deed of 1823 as in occupation of "that large and commodious scribbling and fulling mill lately erected" -------this on a piece of land near Rouse Mill , and mentioning appurtenances and rights to draw water from Wooler Beck to work a steam engine or engines."i,e, Hick Lane Mill.

     Phineas Fox was born on 22 September 1789. He was the son of Phineas Fox and Jane Blakeley. Phineas Fox was baptized on 4 October 1789 at Batley.
He married Elizabeth (?).
In 1818 Phineas Fox is said to have united with Joseph Jubb and John Fox, John Burnley and George Newsome to construct a rag machine.1
Hick Lane Mill
in 1823 He was one of those mentioned in the fourth part deed of 1823 as in occupation of "that large and commodious scribbling and fulling mill lately erected" -------this on a piece of land near Rouse Mill , and mentioning appurtenances and rights to draw water from Wooler Beck to work a steam engine or engines.
From around 1837 Phineas Fox was in a partnership with Joseph Parker , Michael Spedding and James Hall and Leeds cloth merchant Matthew Johnson. The partnership, styled Hall, Johnson, Spedding & Co are said to have been in business at Purlwell Mill.2
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Soothill Lane, Soothill, as a clothier. Also present are Mary Fox, aged 15, relationship unknown, and an apprentice, William (Matthews?).3
Phineas Fox died on 19 February 1847; aged 57.4
