Martha Marshall

#140, b. circa 1780, d. 1868
Martha Marshall|b. c 1780\nd. 1868|p5.htm#i140|Abraham Marshall||p6.htm#i155||||||||||||||||

Relationship=3rd great-grandmother of Vivien Eley.
Charts
Ancestry of Vivien Eley Tomlinson
     Martha Marshall was born circa 1780 at West Ardsley. She was the daughter of Abraham Marshall.
Martha Marshall married John Preston, son of Edward Preston and Hannah Stott, on 22 February 1807 at Birstall.

In the census of 6 June 1841 she was listed as the head of household at Carlinghow, Batley, as a washerwoman. (Something of a puzzle here is the William Blackburn also living with the family). This does not fit with any of the immediate relatives of John Blackburn who her daughter Sarah was later to marry, but could be a more distant connection.1
In the census of 30 March 1851 she was listed as the head of household at Carlinghow, Batley, as an oat cake baker, aged 71.2
In the census of 7 April 1861 she was listed as the head of household at Carlinghow New Hall, Batley, as an oat cake baker and rug maker.3 Carlinghow New Hall seems a stylish residence for Martha's final days, but the census shows four families living there besides herself. Her son-in law John Blackburn is said to have owned the property at one time. This is something I have yet to check out, but it could fit with Martha's residence there.
Martha Preston died in 1868 at Carlinghow.4 She was buried on 25 November 1868 at the cemetery, Batley, aged 91 years.4,5

Martha strikes me as a feisty lady. Seldom at this time do censuses reveal women's occupations in the home, but Martha was clearly not one to let hers go unrecorded.
As a widow, she may have taken in washing as a paid occupation, but I suspect her later occupations were what she saw as her role in the family.Oatcakes were staple fare locally, while the rag rugs she made were probably the "proddy" rugs still common in my childhood.

Relationship=4th great-aunt of Vivien Eley.
     Judith Preston was born on 7 January 1788. She was the daughter of Edward Preston and Hannah Stott. Judith Preston was baptized on 3 February 1788 at Batley.1
She died in 1790. She was buried on 9 July 1890 at Batley.1
