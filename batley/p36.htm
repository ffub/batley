Theodore Cooke Taylor

#1067, b. 3 August 1850, d. 19 October 1952
Theodore Cooke Taylor|b. 3 Aug 1850\nd. 19 Oct 1952|p36.htm#i1067|Joshua Taylor|b. 1821\nd. 19 Jul 1879|p33.htm#i979|Alice Cooke|b. c 1829\nd. 1860|p33.htm#i980|Thomas Taylor|b. 28 Nov 1778\nd. 29 Aug 1857|p33.htm#i981|Rebecca Willans|b. 10 Oct 1783\nd. 3 May 1856|p33.htm#i982|Samuel Cooke||p175.htm#i5091||||

Relationship=4th cousin 4 times removed of Vivien Eley.
Theodore Cooke Taylor. © image courtesy of Batley Community Archive
     Theodore Cooke Taylor was born on 3 August 1850 at Batley. He was the son of Joshua Taylor and Alice Cooke.
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as a scholar aged 10 the son of Joshua Taylor at Healey Bottom, Batley.1
Theodore Cooke Taylor was educated at Batley Grammar School. In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as aged 20 the son of Joshua Taylor at Healey, Batley.2
Theodore Cooke Taylor married Sarah Jane Ingraham in 1874.
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Bottom of Healey, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer, a master.3
In 1884 Theodore Cooke Taylor was appointed a Justice of the Peace.

In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed at Sunny Bank, Upper Batley, as a woollen manufacturer.4
In 1892 Theodore Cooke Taylor bought out the other partners in the firm.

Profit Sharing
He was then able to put into practice his ideas of profit sharing, so that employees might feel they and ownership were "pulling together" for common benefit.
Making the business into a Limited Liability Company in 1896, he was at first the sole director, but in 1918 formed a Board drawn from managerial staff. Seeing full workers control as unwise, shares held by management gave a stake in Company control, whilst those held by others, though with the same financial rights, did not.5


Between 1900 and 1918 Theodore Cooke Taylor was Member of Parliament for the Lancashire Division of Radcliffe -cum-Farnworth. He described his interest in politics as "never partisan, but mainly as one means of improving the lot of mankind."
He married Mary Isabella McVean in 1920, his first wife having died the previous year.
Theodore Cooke Taylor was visited by George Sheard and Douglas Sheard at Cheapside Mill soon after his hundredth birthday. Douglas tells us:-
" I was sitting in the car, and my Father had entered the offices to meet Mr. Taylor. GS was a little early for the appointment, and Mr. Taylor’s limousine rolled up to the door after he had gone in. It was raining, and I personally observed Mr. Taylor get out of the car and climb the approximately 20 steps to the big front door, 2 steps at a time. He was a wiry little man with a grey beard and a twinkle in his very clear eyes. He had taken all his 2500 employees and spouses to Blackpool on buses some 2 weeks before for the celebration of his 100th birthday and had danced with them. He remained active with the company until his sudden, (and unexpected), death in 1952."6

Theodore Cooke Taylor told William's great grandson Douglas Sheard that he had, as a very young man, been in partnership with his great grandfather William Sheard and invested in his enterprise.6

Theodore Cooke Taylor died on 19 October 1952 at Grassington at age 102.

Children of Theodore Cooke Taylor and Sarah Jane Ingraham

  • Evelyn Sarah Taylor b. c 1875, d. 1919
  • Lilian Gertrude Taylor b. 1877
  • Lawrence Ingraham Taylor b. c 1878, d. 1883
