Relationship=1st cousin 5 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     Edward Spedding was said to be deaf and dumb.1 He was born on 6 May 1847.2 He was the son of Michael Spedding and Hannah Butterworth. Edward Spedding was baptized on 31 August 1847 at Batley.2
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the son of Michael Spedding at New Road, Batley.3

Edward Spedding and Elizabeth France Fell were listed in the census of 7 April 1861 at The Deaf and Dumb Institution, Doncaster, as pupils. The couple were later to marry, and it seems likely that this was where they met. This Institution, founded in 1829, was the earliest school for the deaf in Yorkshire. Pupils had been in the way of waving from the school balcony at racegoers, who would throw them coins, though these were rounded up by the headmaster as additional income. However, concerns about the immorailty of racegoing and betting led to a resolution in this year "that the children of the institution be not allowed in future to appear on the balcony during the race week."4
Edward Spedding married Elizabeth France Fell in 1870.
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Taylor Street, Batley, as a stone carver, listed as deaf and dumb from birth.5

Martha Hirst married James Hall, son of Thomas Hall and Frances Allott, on 31 December 1810, at Batley witnessed by (Thos?) Hirst and Jeremiah Chadwick.1
Martha Hall and James Hall were said to have adopted Martha's nephew, William Dean.
In the census of 6 June 1841 Martha Hall was listed at Aldams House, Dewsbury, as wife of James Hall.2
In the census of 30 March 1851 Martha Hall was listed at Purlwell, Batley, as wife of James Hall.3
In the census of 7 April 1861 she was listed as the head of household at Purlwell, Batley, as an annuitant aged 75 living with her brother.4
