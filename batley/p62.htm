Abraham Stubley

#1870, b. 1705
Abraham Stubley|b. 1705|p62.htm#i1870|John Stubley|d. Jan 1713/14|p57.htm#i1663|Susanna (?)|d. Mar 1709/10|p62.htm#i1875|||||||||||||

Relationship=7th great-uncle of Vivien Eley.
     Abraham Stubley was born in 1705. He was the son of John Stubley and Susanna (?). Abraham Stubley was baptized on 5 November 1705 at Birstall.1
In 1737 Abraham Stubley was possibly the Abraham Stubley accused along with a Timothy Popplewell, both of Hawksworth, near Guiseley of stealing sheep from a Henry Houseman of Plumpton and driving them beyond Leeds (in a Birstall direction!) along with their own. Their defence was that they had bought the sheep from an unknown person near Harrogate. We do not know the outcome of the case. Popplewells from Hawksworth later laid charges against a Thomas Stubley that he had stolen one of their father's sheep, and I think this could have been Abraham's brother.2,3
