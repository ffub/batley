Israel Colbeck

#4777, b. 1818, d. 4 December 1891
Israel Colbeck|b. 1818\nd. 4 Dec 1891|p163.htm#i4777|William Colbeck|b. 6 Feb 1783\nd. 19 Oct 1849|p77.htm#i2290|Elizabeth Richardson|b. c 1785\nd. 3 Dec 1856|p106.htm#i3160|Henry Colbeck|b. 1736|p70.htm#i2101|Millicent Nowell|b. 1742|p71.htm#i2126|||||||
     Israel Colbeck was born in 1818.1 He was the son of William Colbeck and Elizabeth Richardson. Israel Colbeck was baptized on 31 March 1818 at Old Chapel Independent, Heckmondwike.2
He married Elizabeth Exley, daughter of Samuel Exley and Elizabeth Nelson, on 3 August 1840 at Batley Israel signed, Elizabeth made her mark.
At the time of marrriage he was described as a machine manufacturer. Witnesses were Samuel Haley and Joseph Chadwick.3,4,5
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Havercroft, Batley, as a machine maker.6
In 1842 Israel Colbeck was listed in a Leeds and district Directory (as Coldbeck, Israel) as a machine maker.7
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Havercroft, N side Commercial St., Batley, as a machine maker employing 9 men and 2 boys.8
In 1858 Israel Colbeck was listed in White's Directory as a machine maker.9
In 1869 Elizabeth Exley and Israel with their family are said to have moved to France, where he established himself as a shoddy merchant.4
He died on 4 December 1891 at his residence at 6 St Roman Road, Rouen, France.4

Children of Israel Colbeck and Elizabeth Exley

  • Edwin Colbeck+ b. 14 Feb 1842, d. 19 Apr 1921
  • Ann Colbeck b. c 1845
