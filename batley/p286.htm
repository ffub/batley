Edward Wilson

#7982, b. 1836, d. 1908

Descendants of Michael Sheard born 1688
     Edward Wilson was born in 1836. He was the son of George Wilson and Mary Driver. Edward Wilson was baptized on 3 July 1836 at Batley.1
Edward Wilson was also known as Ned Wilson. In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the son of George Wilson at Havercroft, Batley.2
Edward Wilson is said to have worked for the firm of Ephraim Fox as a lad.3
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as a rag grinder the son of George Wilson at Havercroft, Batley.4
Edward Wilson married Sarah Peel, daughter of Jeremiah Peel, on 11 October 1856 at Batley.1
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Quarry, Batley, as a Rag Grinder.5
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at New Road Side, Batley, as a Rag Grinder.6

A newspaper account tells us that when John Blackburn commenced business near Berlin the German employees had trouble with the rag machines and Ned Wilson was sent to train them. In that John William Blackburn was to have accompanied him on this trip this is unlikely to refer to John's earlier German venture, and may refer to the mill at Kopenick.

In the event Ned had to make the journey alone, as John William was detained in London. Lack of German was a barrier, and when the latter joined him he greeted him with,
"By gow, its abaht time tha come, I can mak nowt o this lot."
Though not impressed with the hard black bread available there, in the nine months spent in Germany he did adopt some German ways, and returned home with long curly hair. Chaffed by his mates, however, he soon reverted to short back and sides.
Edward is said to have enjoyed his beer, and it may be that there were times when he over indulged, for John Blackburn once offered him a new suit if he were teetotal for a year. In this Ned succeeded and won the suit.
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Healey Road, Batley, as a foreman woollen rag grinder.7
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as the head of household at 18 Cobden Street, Batley, as a foreman Rag Grinder.8
In the census of 31 March 1901 he was listed as the head of household at 18 Cobden St, Batley, as a foreman rag grinder.9
He died in 1908.
