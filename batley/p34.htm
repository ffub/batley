Benjamin Stubley

Benjamin Stubley|b. 1775\nd. b 1841|p34.htm#i1015|Abraham Stubley|b. 1737\nd. 1802|p51.htm#i1505|Noah Eastwood|b. 1739\nd. 1792|p51.htm#i1508|Thomas Stubley|b. 1703|p20.htm#i627|Sarah Williamson|d. 1787|p16.htm#i487|William Eastwood|b. 1708|p76.htm#i2278||||

Relationship=1st cousin 6 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     Benjamin Stubley was born in 1775. He was the son of Abraham Stubley and Noah Eastwood. Benjamin Stubley was baptized on 16 April 1775 at Batley.1
He married Elizabeth Beaumont, daughter of Benjamin Beaumont and Grace Spedding, on 8 August 1799 at Batley witnessed by ----Mayman(?) and Benjamin Beaumont.1
I believe this was the Benjamin Stubley who was listed in Pigots directories as a carrier, offering a daily service to Leeds. Indeed he is listed as a carrier as early as 1804 at his son Benjamon's baptism. Baptismal entries for his other children list him variously as farmer, labourer and clothier, but these are not mutually exclusive. We may note that his son William is listed as a common carrier in the 1841 census after his father's death.2,3
He died before 1841.

     William Stubley was born in 1813. He was the son of Benjamin Stubley and Elizabeth Beaumont. William Stubley was baptized on 28 February 1813 at Parish Church, Batley.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as a common carrier aged 20, the son of Betty Stubley at Havercroft, Batley.2
William Stubley was listed in 1842 as a Carrier to Leeds.3

Samuel Stubley

#1030, b. 1817, d. 7 September 1884
Samuel Stubley|b. 1817\nd. 7 Sep 1884|p34.htm#i1030|Benjamin Stubley|b. 1775\nd. b 1841|p34.htm#i1015|Elizabeth Beaumont|b. 27 Jul 1775|p34.htm#i1022|Abraham Stubley|b. 1737\nd. 1802|p51.htm#i1505|Noah Eastwood|b. 1739\nd. 1792|p51.htm#i1508|Benjamin Beaumont|b. 1750\nd. 1822|p27.htm#i817|Grace Spedding|b. 1750\nd. 1821|p9.htm#i239|

Relationship=2nd cousin 5 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     Samuel Stubley was born in 1817. He was the son of Benjamin Stubley and Elizabeth Beaumont. Samuel Stubley was baptized on 9 November 1817 at Parish Church, Batley.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as an agricultural labourer the son of Betty Stubley at Havercroft, Batley.2
Samuel Stubley married Ruth Robertshaw in 1846.3
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Havercroft Lane, Batley, as a carrier at a mill.4
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at 15 Wellington Street, Batley, as a carrier.5
Samuel Stubley married Elizabeth Micklethwaite in 1874.6
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household as a carrier.
Samuel Stubley died on 7 September 1884as a result of an accident whilst loading a waggon with mungo in the mill yard of J. T. & J. Taylor. through a hook breaking loose he fell off against a wall, and died a few days later from his injuries.7

Joseph Stubley

Relationship=3rd cousin 4 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     Joseph Stubley was born in 1820 at Barnsley. He was the son of John Stubley and Martha Easton. Joseph Stubley was baptized on 19 November 1820 at Barnsley.
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as a woollen spinner the son of Martha Stubley at Back Lane, Batley.1
He was listed in the census of 6 June 1841 at Back Lane, Batley, a woollen spinner.2
Joseph Stubley married Eunice Turner on 6 August 1843 at Wakefield.
He was listed in the census of 30 March 1851 at New Road side, Batley, Woollen spinner.3
Joseph Stubley married Ann Dawson in 1858.
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Albion St., Batley, Woollen rag merchant.4
He was listed in the census of 2 April 1871 at Albion St., Batley, overlooker at woollen mill.5
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Taylor St, Batley, as a woollen overlooker.6
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as the head of household at 5 Taylor St, Batley, overlooker, wastepulling.7

Eunice Turner

#1032, b. circa 1824, d. 1856
