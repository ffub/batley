In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Union Street, Batley, a stonemason.3

In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Wheatcroft, Batley, as a mason, master employing 9 men. He and his wife were living next to his mother-in-law Frances Mortimer.5
He witnessed the marriage of Isaac Binns and Sarah Thurman Robinson on 15 April 1868 at Wesleyan Chapel, Batley.6
John Robinson died on 1 March 1870 at Batley. He was buried at the Cemetery, Batley.

     Joseph Stubley was born on 9 April 1797 at Batley.1 He was the son of Joshua Stubley and Mary Blackburn. Joseph Stubley was baptized on 9 July 1797 at Batley.1
He married Frances Smith, daughter of Richard Smith and Grace Hall, on 25 December 1817 at Batley Both made their marks. Witnesses were Robert Brearley and J Chadwick.1
Joseph Stubley died on 4 August 1828 at Batley at age 31. He was buried on 7 August 1828 at Batley.1

     Frances Smith was born in 1798 at Batley. She was the daughter of Richard Smith and Grace Hall. Frances Smith was baptized on 27 July 1798 at Batley.1
She married Joseph Stubley, son of Joshua Stubley and Mary Blackburn, on 25 December 1817 at Batley. Both made their marks. Witnesses were Robert Brearley and J Chadwick.1
Frances Smith was left a widow with five children on the death of Joseph Stubley on 4 August 1828 at Batley.

Frances Smith married Thomas Mortimer on 23 May 1830 at Tong.
In the census of 6 June 1841 Frances Mortimer was listed at Clerk Green, Batley, as wife of Thomas Mortimer.2
She was listed as the head of household in the 30 March 1851 census at Purlwell, Batley, aged 52.3
She was listed as the head of household in the 7 April 1861 census at Wheat Croft, Batley, aged 63.4

Frances Smith died on 4 April 1865 at Batley. She was buried on 6 April 1865 at Batley with both her husbands.1

     Frances Stubley was born in 1828. She was the daughter of Joseph Stubley and Frances Smith. Frances Stubley was baptized on 2 March 1828 at Parish Church, Batley.1
She married William Mawson, son of George Mawson, on 13 July 1845 at Batley witnesses John Mitchell, Jos chadwick.1
In the census of 30 March 1851 Frances Mawson was listed at Clerk Green, Batley, as wife of William Mawson.2
In the census of 7 April 1861 she was listed as a rag sorter the daughter of Frances Mortimer at Wheat Croft, Batley.3
In the census of 2 April 1871 she was listed as the head of household at High Street, Batley, as a grocer.4
Frances Stubley married Benjamin Asquith, a colliery manager, in 1881.
In the census of 5 April 1891 she was listed as the head of household at Asquith's Buildings, Wakefield Road,, Drighlington, as a widow aged 63, retired. A grandaughter, Mary E Mawson aged 17 is also present.5
