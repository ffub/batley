Henry Firth

#8112, b. circa 1832
     Henry Firth was born circa 1832. He was listed in the census of 30 March 1851 working as a farm servant for John Marshall, a farmer of 99 acres at Cawood Road, Sherburn, Yorkshire.1
He married Sarah (?).
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Oakwell Hall, Birstall, as an agricultural labourer.2
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Oakwell, Birstall, as a groom.3
Henry was apparently already working for George Sheard as he was living adjacently.
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Nook, Batley, as a coachman. A nephew, Albert R Saynor is also with the family.4
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as the head of household at Woodlands Lodge, Batley, as a coachman, domestic.He would have had charge of the coach house and stabling for four horses, with harness room and what was described as a carriage washing shed.5,6
In the census of 31 March 1901 he was listed as the head of household at I Regent Street, Bingley, as a retired coachman, domestic.7
Henry Firth was one of the coffin bearers at George Sheard's funeral.

     James Davis was born circa 1861 at Hanbury, Worcestershire. He was the son of James Davis.
I can only find one possible James Davis born in Hanbury, the son of a gamekeeper of the same name.
He was listed in the census of 3 April 1881 at Daisy Hill, Rawdon, Leeds, working as one of several general labouresr at a substantial property where domestic and garden staff were kept.1
James Davis married Mary (?).
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as the head of household at Woodlands Lodge, Batley, as a gentleman's gardener. The gardener's cottage also served as entrance lodge.2
James Davis is housed in the lodge which also served as entrance lodge; Both coachman Henry and new gardener. In the census of 31 March 1901 he was listed as the head of household at Woodlands Lodge, Batley.3
James Davis was must have left George Sheard's employ soon after this.
He was one of the coffin bearers at George Sheard's funeral.
