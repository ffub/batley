     George Brearley was born in 1813. He was the son of Robert Brearley and Dorothy Hall. George Brearley was baptized on 28 February 1813 at Batley.

In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Clerk Green, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer.2
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Clerk Green, Batley, as a woollen cloth manufacturer, firm of 8 employing 30 men, 3 women, 10 boys and 11 girls.3
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Killen, Batley, as a woollen cloth fuller aged 48.4
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Dark Lane, Batley, No occupation is given. Also present is a grandson, Herbert Brearley, aged 5.5
George Brearley married Ann Terry, daughter of Mark Terry and Elizabeth Ineson, on 1 September 1874 at Batley.1
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Wellington Street, Batley, as a retired cloth manufacturer.6
He was listed in the census of 5 April 1891 at 17 No 2 Yard, Dark Lane, Batley, as a widow aged 41 living on own means.7 George Brearley died in 1891. He was buried on 21 December 1891 at Batley Cemetery ; as a manufacturer, of Dark Lane.8 George Brearley's grave is No A168 on the unconsecrated side.

     Francis Senior was born in 1827 at Batley. He was the son of Squire Senior and Frances Hall.
Francis Senior was baptized on 16 April 1827 at Batley.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as as a woollen piecer the son of Squire Senior at New Batley.2
Francis Senior married Mary Sheard, daughter of David Sheard and Martha Talbot, on 17 September 1848 at Batley.1
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at New Road, Batley, as a spinner.3
Francis Senior was to follow his sister Isabella and her husband Jeremiah Mitchell who had emigrated to America in 1855.4 Francis and Mary emigrated on 18 August 1856 sailing on the Ontario from Liverpool with their two children and Mary's half-brother; Matilda Senior, James S Senior and Titus Sheard .Titus went with them, apparently, as a last minute decision at his pleading. They landed at Castle Garden, New York on September 29th after a 6 week voyage.5
Francis Senior was joined by another sister Ellen Bailey and her husband Charles Bailey in 1857 at Little Falls, New York.6 In the census of 7 July 1860 he was listed as the head of household at Little Falls, Herkimer, New York, as a factory hand.7
Francis Senior died in 1864.8

Children of Francis Senior and Mary Sheard

  • Matilda Senior b. 19 Nov 1853
  • James S Senior+ b. Mar 1856, d. 1913
  • Francis M Senior+ b. 24 May 1862, d. 4 Aug 1930

     Benjamin Stubley was born in 1832 at Batley. He was the son of Grace Stubley. Benjamin Stubley was baptized on 2 September 1832 at Batley.1
He appeared aged 8 on the census of 6 June 1841 in the household of his grandmother Betty Stubley at Havercroft, Batley.2 In the census of 30 Mar 1851 he was listed as a cloth dresser the nephew of Noah Stubley.3

Benjamin Stubley married Jane Sheard, daughter of David Sheard and Martha Talbot, on 7 February 1853 Witnesses were Joe Webster and Francis Senior.4,1
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Croslee st(?), Batley, as a rag wool merchant employing 3(?) hands.5
He was listed in the census of 2 April 1871 at King Street, Batley, as a rag and flock dealer employing 2 hands.6
In 1878 Benjamin Stubley was listed as a rag merchant at Bradford Road, Batley.7
He was listed in the census of 3 April 1881 at Bradford Road Park House, Batley, as a rag merchant employing 2 hands.8
He was listed in the census of 5 April 1891 at Cheapside Hill, Batley, as a rag merchant.9
He died on 14 August 1892 at Batley.  And was buried at the cemetery, Batley.
