Martha Newsome née Preston © image courtesy of Ian Bolton
     Martha Preston was born in 1854 at Batley. She was the daughter of William Preston and Grace Gledhill. Martha Preston was baptized on 24 December 1854 at Batley.1
In the census of 7 April 1861 she was listed as a scholar the daughter of William Preston at Victoria Street, Batley.2
In the census of 2 April 1871 she was listed as a weaver, woollen the daughter of William Preston at Church Lane, Batley.3
Martha Preston married Joseph Newsome, son of William Newsome and Emma Collinson, on 14 June 1873 at The Parish Church, Batley.4,5
In the census of 3 April 1881 Martha Newsome was listed at Carlinghow Hill, Batley, as wife of Joseph Newsome.6
In the census of 5 April 1891 Martha Newsome was listed at 5 Carlinghow Hill, Batley, as wife of Joseph Newsome.7
In the census of 2 April 1911 Martha Newsome was listed at 35 Cross Bank Road, Batley, as wife of Joseph Newsome.8
In 1945 Martha Newsome celebrated her ninetieth birthday, and at this time her youngest daughter Amy was living with her.
Some of the interesting photos Ian Bolton has sent me are badly damaged, and I was puzzled by the discrepancy between this and the way in which they were clearly valued by the family. The explanation was not one I would have guessed at; the damage occurred when his grandfather's house was destroyed by a cyclone in 1971! Although I have removed a few bad scratches from other photos, I have not before attempted so much of a restoration exercise as on these, and on this, perhaps the worst affected, I include a before. and after shot. Though damage still shows around Martha's eyes, and I can only add it to the website at a low resolution, this perhaps represents better a charming photo of some quality of Martha and her youngest daughter Amy.

  • Clara Newsome b. 1877
