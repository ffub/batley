Peter Riley

#7375, b. 15 July 1858, d. 10 November 1929
Peter Riley|b. 15 Jul 1858\nd. 10 Nov 1929|p263.htm#i7375|Robinson Riley|b. 1834|p264.htm#i7392||||Abraham Riley||p265.htm#i7439||||||||||
      Peter Riley was also known as Peter Rylah. He was born on 15 July 1858 at Earlsheaton.1 He was the son of Robinson Riley.
Peter Riley married Emma Brearley, daughter of John Brearley and Hannah Cropper, on 6 June 1881 at The Parish Church, Batley, witnesses were Fred Hodgson and Emma's sister Martha Ann Brearley.1
Peter Riley emigrated in 1884. It seems probable that he was the Peter Riley described as a factory hand with one item of baggage, planning to settle in the USA, who sailed on the Borderer from Liverpool, arriving in Boston on the 11th June.2
In the census of 7 June 1900 he was listed as the head of household at South Main St, Burrillville, Providence, Rhode Island, as a woollen weaver. Lodging with the family at this time are William Brearley, a dye hand, probably Emma's brother although the date of birth given as 1872 is wrong, and a Daniel Carter aged 69, both of English origin.3
In the census of 28 April 1910 he was listed as the head of household at 213 Broad Street, Burrillville, Providence, Rhode Island, as a weaver, woollen mill. Living a few door away are the Daughtery family, also from Batley.4
In the census of 7 January 1920 he was listed as the head of household at 180 Broad Street, Burrillville, Providence, Rhode Island, now a widower of no occupation.5
He died on 10 November 1929 at Burrillville, Providence, Rhode Island, at age 71.6

George Daughtery

#7384, b. circa 1826
     George Daughtery was born circa 1826 at Dewsbury. He married Hannah Fox, daughter of Charles Fox and Mary Newsome, on 6 September 1846 at Dewsbury Hannah is as of Batley.1
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Havercroft, Batley, as a cloth fuller (here as Daltry, or Dalby in Ancestry).2
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Bank Foot, Batley, as a cloth fuller.3
