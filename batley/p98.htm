James Colbeck

#2955, b. 1820, d. 1914
James Colbeck|b. 1820\nd. 1914|p98.htm#i2955|Joseph Colbeck|b. 15 Feb 1787\nd. 1 Jul 1855|p70.htm#i2119|Sarah Constantine|b. c 1787\nd. 1 May 1875|p103.htm#i3089|Joseph Colbeck|b. 13 Jan 1761\nd. 1833|p70.htm#i2113|Ann Lindley|b. 1761\nd. 1835|p70.htm#i2114|||||||
     James Colbeck was born in 1820 in a house near the Lord Nelson Inn at Carlnghow, Batley.
He was the son of Joseph Colbeck and Sarah Constantine. James Colbeck was baptized on 26 November 1820 at Batley.
He was educated at Batley Grammar School
; from the age of seven. In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the son of Joseph Colbeck at Carlinghow, Batley.1
James Colbeck married Amelia Garret, daughter of Joseph Garret, on 31 July 1845.2
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Carlinghow, Batley, as a cloth weaver.3
James Colbeck was employed by John Blackburn for a time as a foreman. In five years his wages went up from 15s per week to 30s per week. Working hours were long, not finishing until 7 or 8 pm on weekdays and 4 or 5 pm on Saturdays.4
He married Rachel Talbot, daughter of John Talbot and Hannah Slater, on 19 February 1858 at Batley.2,5
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Albert Terrace, Dewsbury, as a manager in a woollen mill.6
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Eyre Street, Batley, as manager of a woollen manufactory.
James Colbeck died in 1914; aged 93.7

  • Eliza Colbeck b. 20 Feb 1854, d. 1855

Thomas Shepley

#2958, d. August 1596
Thomas Shepley|d. Aug 1596|p98.htm#i2958|an unknown Shepley||p165.htm#i4832||||||||||||||||

Relationship=11th great-grandfather of Vivien Eley.
Charts
Ancestry of Vivien Eley Tomlinson
     Thomas Shepley was the son of an unknown Shepley.  Unknown because he was born before Parish Records began for Mirfield, which was in September 1559.
Two children are registered as born in Mirfield, but we know that there were others from later mentions.
We also know that he had a brother, Robert from references in Robert's will.
His brother Robert died in 1571, and in his will he mentions Edward, Thomas and Isabel, and their sisters, children of his brother.
At this date it is often easier to find out more about wealthier relations who may make wills or be recorded in other transactions, and these Shepleys certainly appear among these.
Much of what we know about Thomas comes from his own will made on 17 June 1596 Described as Thomas Shepley the Elder, yeoman, he asks that he be buried in Mirfield Church or churchyard, and leaves ten shillings to the local poor and twenty shillings to the mending of the highways. There are also bequests to all his servants, singling out one, Alice Sykes, who receives a larger sum of £3.6s 8d.
We learn that he is possessed of leases of fulling mills, one on the River Calder near the Queens's corn mills at Wakefield, others described as near to Wakefield, and one at Alverthorpe. These are left in trust for Thomas, Edward and Richard, sons of his younger son Edward Shepley.1 Although his elder son Thomas was an executor of the will, it sets out provision for his brother's sons; Presumably provision was otherwise made for his elder son. Thomas Shepley died in August 1596 at Mirfield. He was buried on 21 August 1596 at Mirfield.2

  • Margaret Shepley
  • Isabell Shepley
  • Elizabeth Shepley d. 1574
  • Doritha Shepley b. 1561, d. 1561

Robert Shepley

#2959, d. 1571
Robert Shepley|d. 1571|p98.htm#i2959|an unknown Shepley||p165.htm#i4832||||||||||||||||

Relationship=12th great-uncle of Vivien Eley.
     Robert Shepley was the son of an unknown Shepley.   Unknown because he was born before Parish Records began for Mirfield, which was in September 1559.
We also know that he had a brother, Thomas from references in his will.

Robert Shepley made a will on 18 May 1571 at the time he was living with John Walker whose wife was a Shepley, possibly his sister.
A colourful detail is his mention of his "cloke, beste hose, dublet and jacket" left to John Walker, whilst other bequests include "my workeday jacket and dublet" to an Edward France.1 In his will he mentions Edward, Thomas and Isabel, and their sisters, children of his brother Thomas.
Robert Shepley died in 1571 at Mirfield. He was buried on 23 May 1571 at Mirfield.2
