John Burnley

#365, b. 14 July 1779, d. 2 January 1836
John Burnley|b. 14 Jul 1779\nd. 2 Jan 1836|p12.htm#i365|Michael Burnley|b. 1748|p11.htm#i353|Ruth Blakeley|b. 1752|p11.htm#i352|John Burnley||p288.htm#i8034||||Matthew Blakeley|b. 1726|p11.htm#i350||||

Relationship=3rd cousin 5 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     John Burnley was born on 14 July 1779. He was the son of Michael Burnley and Ruth Blakeley. John Burnley was baptized on 25 July 1779 at Batley.
He married Elizabeth Blakeley, daughter of John Blakeley and Sarah Swallow, on 2 November 1801 at Batley witnesses Abraham Blakeley and George Newsome.1
John Burnley according to a newspaper account, set up a rag machine in his barn, worked by donkey power, and in competition with Benjamin Law's early experiments.2
Another account says that it was in 1818John Burnley that he got together with Joseph Jubb and John Fox, Phineas Fox and George Newsome to construct a rag machine.3

Hick Well Mill

Willans tells us that a few years after Hick Lane Mill was built,
"Mr John Burnley, an enterprising Batley tradesman, built another mill not far from the Hick Lane Mill, and carried on a large trade in the Batley special line, such as druggets, pilots, &c., his sons going into the business as well."4
John Burnley made a will on 24 December 1835; mentions wife Elizabeth and sons John, Abraham, Samuel and Jacob, the latter still a minor.Also mentions daughter Fanny, wife of Robert Scargill and mentions nephew Matthew, son of his brother Matthew, which helps confirm his identity.5
He died on 2 January 1836aged 56.
He was buried on 6 January 1836 at Batley.1
The death of John Burnley in 1836 saw his heirs pulling out of the Hick Lane Mill , leaving Michael Spedding, Michael Sheard, Joseph Jubb and Phineas and David Fox.6
