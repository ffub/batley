     Mary Brooke was born circa 1835. She was the daughter of William Brooke and Sarah Sheard. In the census of 6 June 1841 she was listed as aged 5 the daughter of William Brooke at Havercroft, Batley.1
In the census of 30 March 1851 she was listed as aged 14, scholar the daughter of William Brooke at Havercroft, Batley.2
Mary Brooke married James Ineson, son of John Ineson, on 3 May 1855 at Birstall deduced from fact that son W. J. Ineson's obituary describes himself as grandson of William Brooke, whose business he and his brother took over on William's death.3,4,5
In the census of 2 April 1871 she was listed as the head of household at Bradford Road, Batley, as a rag merchant. She was said by her son to have carried on his late father's business for a while, despite the fact that it was not quite suitable for a woman to manage.4
