Mark Blackburn

#3326, b. 23 September 1846, d. 4 January 1924
Mark Blackburn|b. 23 Sep 1846\nd. 4 Jan 1924|p112.htm#i3326|Thomas Blackburn|b. 1823|p2.htm#i65|Elizabeth Terry|b. 1824\nd. 17 Dec 1888|p43.htm#i1269|William Blackburn|b. c 1800\nd. 26 Jul 1849|p2.htm#i63|Sarah Thompson|b. 1 May 1801\nd. Relationship=1st cousin 3 times removed of Vivien Eley.
Charts
Descendants of William Blackburn
      Mark Blackburn was also known as Mark Terry. He was born on 23 September 1846.1 He was the son of Thomas Blackburn and Elizabeth Terry. Mark Blackburn was baptized as Mark Terry on 27 December 1846 at the Parish Church, Batley.1

In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the son of Sarah Blackburn but I believe him to be the child baptised as Mark Terry born to Elizabeth Terry, now listed as Elizabeth Blackburn, before her marriage to Thomas Blackburn.2
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as a worker in a rag warehouse the son of Elizabeth Blackburn at Peel St, Batley.3
Mark Blackburn married Anna Lorenz.4
Mark Blackburn has not been found in later censuses in England, and he is believed to have spent the rest of his life in Germany. He must be the M Blackburn listed in Berlin directories in the early 1880s as a bookkeeper, and it is likely that he was working in this capacity for his uncle John, but from 1885 we find him listed as a merchant.5
He took over part of his uncle John Blackburn's business interests in Berlin circa 1891.

1898 Mark Blackburn was listed as a member of the Berlin Sailing club along with other Blackburns including his uncle Charles.6
Mark Blackburn died on 4 January 1924 at Wilhelmshavener Strasse 66, Berlin, at age 77.7

Child of Mark Blackburn and Anna Lorenz

  • Elizabeth Charlotte Blackburn b. 6 Aug 1875

Mary Brook

#3327, b. circa 1804, d. 27 October 1857
     Mary Brook was born circa 1804 at Batley.
Mary Brook married Joseph Senior on 17 September 1825 at Batley. Witnesses were Benjamin Peel and James Brook.1
Mary Senior married Joseph Jubb, son of John Jubb, on 19 April 1832 at Wakefield They were married by licence as Joseph Jubb of Batley and Mary Senior of the parish of Wakefield. Witnesses were Benjamin Spedding and Jane Senior.2,3
In the census of 6 June 1841 Mary Brook was listed as wife of Joseph Jubb.4
In the census of 30 March 1851 Mary Brook was listed at Havercroft, Batley, as wife of Joseph Jubb.5
Mary Brook died on 27 October 1857 at Batley; aged 54.6 She was listed aged 56 on the census of 7 April 1861 at Commercial Street, Batley, as wife of Joseph Jubb.7
