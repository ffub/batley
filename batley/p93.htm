In 1742 Jeremiah Shepley was a tithe gatherer, and it must have been in this connection that he prepared the map shown here, surveyed for John Copley Esquire.

Joseph Bigland

#2824
     A marriage bond referring to Joseph Bigland of St Crux, York aged 26 and Elizabeth Shipley of Batley, 25, gives a marriage date of 29 August 1721 at Batley, Dewsbury or Wakefield Parish Church.
Joseph Bigland married Elizabeth Shepley, daughter of Marmaduke Shepley and Anne Eton, on 28 September 1721 at The Parish Church, Batley Described as Joseph Bigland of the Parish of St Cross in York and Elizabeth Shepley of Batley, married by Licence.1
The will of Elizabeth's father Marmaduke Shepley leaves forty pounds to his daughter Elizabeth Bigland, to be left to her or her husband Joseph Bigland within two years after his decease, provided that she or a child of hers is alive at that time.2
We learn of Joseph and Elizabeth's daughters from the will of their grandmother Ann Shepley signed on 8 April 1734 in which she leaves six pounds to be divided between them and her other grandaughter, Mary Shepley.
Joseph Bigland described as of the city of York in the will of his brother-in-law Jeremiah Shepley in 1745.
A Mr Bigland is listed in a valuation of property in Batley for poor Rate purposes as tenant of 4 acres I rood and 20 perches of land in Batley valued at £2 13s 10d in 1756.2
