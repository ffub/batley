      Jonas Sheard was also known as James Jonas Sheard.1 He was born on 11 September 1849. He was the son of William Sheard and Sarah Ann Newsome. Jonas Sheard was baptized on 28 October 1849 at Batley.1
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as a scholar, the son of William Sheard at Albert St, Batley.2
According to T.C. Taylor, who describes him as an old schoolfellow Jonas Sheard was educated at the Grammar School at the Grammar School, Batley.3

He travelled in 1868 from Liverpool to New York, on the James Foster Junior, described as a dyer aged 19.4
He was back in England to marry Hannah Roberts, daughter of Oliver Roberts, on 20 September 1869 at the Register Office, Dewsbury, witnwsses were Cha: Carr and Chas Martin.
Learning his trade from his father, William, he was privy to the secrets of the business. At the age of 19 or 20 he was purported to have abandoned the business and have stolen/copied/taken the formulae to the USA and to Germany, which was at that time a major competitor to England in aniline dyestuffs. He was thought to have sold them or worked for a short time in Germany, and was lost track of by the family".5
It is hard to know how to square this with Jonas's recorded travels to America. It may be rather that he took the secrets there, but I think it quite possible that he went to Germany sometime in the early 1870s. It will be seen from my account of John Blackburn that there were Batley contacts in Berlin.
If son George was indeed born in Pennsylvania, then there was another trip there and home again for the birth of Ralph. All later children are shown as born in America.
In the census of 1880 he was listed as the head of household at 3rd Dist, Johnston,, Providence, Rhode Island, as a dyer, woollen mill, born 1850.6
In the census of 16 February 1892 he was listed as the head of household at Little Falls, Herkimer, New York.7
In the census of 8 June 1900 he was listed as the head of household at Cleveland Township, Ohio, as a dyer. Date of immigration given as 1875.8
In the census of 22 April 1910 he was listed as the head of household at Stayton, Marion, Oregon, as a dyer aged 60.9
In the census of 2 January 1920 Jonas Sheard appeared as a lodger in the household of Clark P Leslie and Clark P Leslie, a blacksmith at Santa Ana Township, Orange, California, Jonas was listed as a dyer in a woollen mill. His year of immigration was here given as 1868.10
In 1922 Jonas Sheard was listed as a dyer and a Republican voter, living at 130 W 3rd St., Long Beach City Precinct No 2, Los Angesles, California

; listed as a dyer and a Republican voter, living at.11 This must be the Jonas who wrote to T C Taylor from California in 1930 as follows:-

"Colbeck Bros. had a dyehouse at Cheapside Mills and my father was the first dyer. He dyed in a cast-iron dyepan with a winch turned by hand. As he was on piecework, my mother turned the winch by hand for him and she had me a baby wrapped in a shawl in the cart used to carry the cloth, so it is no wonder I have been a dyer all my life."12
