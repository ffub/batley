John Stubley

#963, b. 14 April 1850, d. 21 April 1911
John Stubley|b. 14 Apr 1850\nd. 21 Apr 1911|p32.htm#i963|James Stubley|b. 6 Jun 1824\nd. 3 Sep 1896|p33.htm#i983|Sarah Fox|b. 2 Feb 1824\nd. 7 Jul 1885|p15.htm#i447|John Stubley|b. 3 Jun 1796\nd. 1839|p33.htm#i990|Martha Easton||p33.htm#i991|David Fox|b. 24 May 1784\nd. 11 Apr 1850|p14.htm#i429|Mary Ibberson|b. 11 Dec 1789\nd. 10 Dec 1826|p14.htm#i430|

Relationship=3rd cousin 4 times removed of Vivien Eley.
John Stubley as Mayor of Batley: collection V. E. Tomlinson
     John Stubley was born on 14 April 1850. He was the son of James Stubley and Sarah Fox.
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as as a scholar aged 10 the son of James Stubley at Albert Street, Batley.1
John Stubley was educated at Batley Grammar School and later at Silcoates School.
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as a woollen manufacturer the son of James Stubley at Albert Terrace, Batley.2
John Stubley married Adeline Taylor, daughter of Joshua Taylor and Alice Cooke, on 13 June 1875 at the Congregational Church, Batley.
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Grange Villa, Batley Field Hill, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer aged 30.3
He was to be found in the census of 5 April 1891 at Hazelwood Hydropathic Establishment, Grange over Sands, apparently taking the waters.4
In 1896 brothers John and David Stubley were in charge of the Batley mills following the death of their father in that year.
John Stubley was an office bearer in Hanover Street Congregational Church.5
In the census of 31 March 1901 he was listed as the head of household at Carlton Grange, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer.6
In 1909 John Stubley was living at The Uplands, Batley, purchased in that year.
John Stubley was elected Mayor in 1909 on the enlargement of the Borough, by the inclusion therein of Soothill Upper, an outlying district, and for a second term in 1910-11, the Coronation Year of George V.7
He was Elected a Batley Alderman in 1910.5
He was Member of National Liberal, London, and Batley Liberal Clubs.5
He died on 21 April 1911 at age 61.
