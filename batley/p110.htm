Isaac Sheard © image courtesy of Jackie Chontas, Roberta Florczyk and James Giedris
     Isaac Sheard was born on 29 January 1868 at Ravensthorpe. He was the son of Jacob Sheard and Ann Naylor.
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as aged 3 the son of Jacob Sheard at Inman Fold, Ravensthorpe.1
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as a scholar aged 13 the son of Jacob Sheard at Albion Street, Mirfield.2
In 1883 Isaac Sheard emigrated to Little Falls, New York State, with his parents Jacob and Ann Sheard.3,4 Isaac Sheard had a rough time in his late teens, when a newspaper report tells us that only a few days after losing two fingers in an accident at Titus Sheard's mill, he was badly kicked whilst attempting to pass a team of oxen in Little Falls and suffered a broken leg.5

Isaac Sheard married Martha Duesler, daughter of Edward C Duesler and Elizabeth Christman, on 14 February 1891.6
In the census of 11 June 1900 he was listed as the head of household at 7 Webster Street, Little Falls, Herkimer, New York, working as a spinner.7
This must have been at his great uncle Titus Sheard's Eagle knitting mill where his obituary tells us he was employed for 20 years.
In the census of 1910 he was listed as the head of household at 10 Webster Street, Litttle Falls, Herkimer, New York, in a knitting machine factory.8
In the census of 12 January 1920 he was listed as the head of household at Little Falls, Herkimer, New York, (His occupation is hard to read, but looks to me like shipper clerk, machine shop) His obituary adds that he worked in Stafford and Holt machine shops on Mill Street.9
In the census of 5 April 1930 he was listed as the head of household at 64 Jackson Street, Little Falls, Herkimer, New York,, by now aged 62 and as of no occupation.10
Isaac Sheard died on 25 February 1935 at Jackson Street, Little Falls, New York, at age 67.6

  • Jessie Sheard+ b. 8 Jul 1898, d. 14 Mar 1940
  • Arleen Martha Sheard b. c 1903
