     Benjamin Sheard was born on 21 December 1777 at Batley. He was the son of George Sheard and Hannah Blakeley. Benjamin Sheard was baptized on 8 February 1778 at Batley.
He married Elizabeth Chappel, daughter of Joseph Chappel and Mary Peel, on 22 December 1798 at Batley witnessed by George Sheard and Jeremiah Chadwick.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Havercroft, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer.2
Benjamin Sheard died on 20 January 1847 at age 69. He this is the Benjamin recorded as serving in the 3rd West Yorkshire Militia from 1803 to 1829, having enlisted for 25 years.
He is recorded as having been discharged with contagious opthalmia caught on duty at Norman Cross, and having imperfect vision.
He served as: Private 205 days
Corporal 7 years
Sergeant 18 years
He was 5' 6" , grey hair, hazel eyes, swarthy complexion

The 3rd West York Militia do not appear to have seen any active service, but were stationed at various towns around the country before being sent to Ireland. In 1803 they were "reviewed on the Town Moor by the Right Honourable the Earl FitzWilliam. The Regiment marched on the 27th May to York."13 Later in the year they were at the Coxheath Encampment, being reviewed on Thursday 30th August by the Earl of Chatham; "the leisure hours allowed the men were employed in the various amusements of cricket, balls, races etc. On Friday August 31st ... a grand Cricket Match."14 In 1806 they were stationed at Hull, marching from there on April 29th on route for Darlington and Durham. By Friday October 3rd they were leaving Newcastle for Sunderland. They appear to have been stationed here for a year, marching on Monday October 12th 1807 on their route to Liverpool. In November 1808 they were quartered at Norman Cross Barracks. Ireland seems to have been the next destination, and in 1813 "The 3rd West York Militia were inspected at Cove, and forts in Cork Harbour ... before the departure from Ireland."
( notes from Hatfield, Historical notices of Doncaster, 1866)
NB Hannah Maria SHEARD b Faversham 1805. I have found mention of an officer's child also baptised 1805 Faversham.
Coxheath encampment was near Maidstone.3

     Letitia Newsome was born on 8 June 1796. She was the daughter of Abraham Newsome and Hannah Sheard. Letitia Newsome was baptized on 2 July 1797 at Batley.1
She married Joseph Chappel on 5 October 1817 at Batley witnessed by Mark Pyrah and David Fox.1
Letitia Newsome married Leonard Stubley, son of Joseph Stubley and Ann Annowey, on 21 March 1830 at Batley witnessed by Joseph Chadwick and Mark Pyrah.1
In the census of 30 March 1851 she was listed as the head of household at Havercroft, Batley, a widow aged 55. Also present is a grandaughter Mary Chappel.2
Letitia Stubleyis I believe. the one who appeared as grandmother-in-law, formerly a midwife, on the census of 2 April 1871 in the household of George Pyrah Senior and Sarah Senior at Providence Street, Batley; as a cloth dresser.3 Letitia Stubley died in 1875. She was buried on 14 February 1875 ; as of Providence St.4
