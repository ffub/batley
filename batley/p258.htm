Edward Bailey

#7261, b. circa 1835
Edward Bailey|b. c 1835|p258.htm#i7261|David Bailey|b. c 1796|p258.htm#i7256|Sarah (?)|b. c 1796|p258.htm#i7257|||||||||||||
     Edward Bailey was born circa 1835 at Oldham, Lancashire. He was the son of David Bailey and Sarah (?). In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as a piecener the son of David Bailey at Hanging Heaton.1
Edward Bailey married Mary Heppleston, daughter of Robert Heppleston and Ellen Senior, on 14 June 1856.2
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Albion Street, Batley, as a woollen rag dealer.3
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Jubb's Mill Yard as an engine driver.4
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Jubb's Mill Yard, Batley, as engineer in mill.5

On 23 August 1884 An account in the Batley News tells us that Edward, who until some three months ago had been working as engineer at Victoria Mills, Carlinghow, had left to take up a similar post at Perseverance Mill, Niederschönweide. In the course of work being done on the mill premises he is said to have fallen into a boiler flue and been severely injured, though his condition was improving.6
A further account the following week from a Mr R H Gates at Schonweide on behalf of John Blackburn, claims that although his engineer Edward did fall the previous week's account was ridiculously exaggerated.6
It would be nice to think that this was the case, but I have not been able to find out more. Edward must have died before 1891 when his wife is listed as a widow.
