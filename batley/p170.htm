Elizabeth France Fell

#4974, b. circa 1848
     Elizabeth France Fell was born circa 1848 at Bradley. She and Edward Spedding were listed in the census of 7 April 1861 at The Deaf and Dumb Institution, Doncaster, as pupils. The couple were later to marry, and it seems likely that this was where they met. This Institution, founded in 1829, was the earliest school for the deaf in Yorkshire. Pupils had been in the way of waving from the school balcony at racegoers, who would throw them coins, though these were rounded up by the headmaster as additional income. However, concerns about the immorailty of racegoing and betting led to a resolution in this year "that the children of the institution be not allowed in future to appear on the balcony during the race week."1
Elizabeth France Fell married Edward Spedding, son of Michael Spedding and Hannah Butterworth, in 1870.
Elizabeth France Fell was listed as deaf and dumb on the census of 2 April 1871 at Taylor Street, Batley, as wife of Edward Spedding.2

Children of Elizabeth France Fell and Edward Spedding

  • Thomas Robert Spedding b. 4 Aug 1873, d. 1875
