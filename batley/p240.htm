Squire Woffenden

#6773, b. 21 January 1784
Squire Woffenden|b. 21 Jan 1784|p240.htm#i6773|William Woffenden|b. 28 Jul 1758\nd. 17 Jan 1830|p28.htm#i852|Grace Allott|b. 1761\nd. Relationship=1st cousin 6 times removed of Vivien Eley.
      Squire Woffenden was also known as Squire Wovenden. He was born on 21 January 1784.1 He was the son of William Woffenden and Grace Allott. Squire Woffenden was baptized on 11 April 1784 at Batley.1
He joined the Army, serving in the 34th Regiment through the Peninsular War. He attained the rank of Sergeant Major, and was awarded a Campaign Medal with 8 clasps representing battles he had fought in.2 Squire Woffenden heard of Catherine Exley's presence in Ireland and was ready to contact her with a remittance to help her reach her husband Joshua, also of his regiment and from Batley, when she arrived where he was located.
Squire Woffenden married Jane (?).
On 21 September 1829 Squire Woffenden was was one of the first recruits in the Metropolitan Police founded by Sir Robert Peel in that year, his warrant number being 679. Records show him as being attached to F division.
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Berwick Row, Miller St, Manchester, as a cloth maker aged 55.3
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Miller St, Manchester, as a pensioner, Chelsy (sic).4
