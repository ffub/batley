Nathaniel Arthur Wilson

#7275, b. 18 May 1886, d. 11 August 1953
Nathaniel Arthur Wilson|b. 18 May 1886\nd. 11 Aug 1953|p259.htm#i7275|Joseph William Wilson||p259.htm#i7274|Tabitha Ann Ineson|b. 24 Apr 1859|p160.htm#i4690|||||||Benjamin Ineson|b. 22 Dec 1833|p153.htm#i4525|Elizabeth Hall|b. c 1834|p153.htm#i4526|

Relationship=4th cousin 2 times removed of Vivien Eley.
Charts
Descendants of Joseph Hall
commemorative image from a New Zealand phone card
      Nathaniel Arthur Wilson also went by the name of Ranji Wilson. He was born on 18 May 1886 at Christchurch, New Zealand.1 He was the son of Joseph William Wilson and Tabitha Ann Ineson. Nathaniel Arthur Wilson was was an early member of the All Blacks, playing against an Anglo-Welsh touring team in 1908, and against Australia in 1910, 1913 and 1914. During the First World War he was a star of the New Zealand services team which won the Kin's Cup against other dominion countries in 1918-1919, but was unable to tour South Africa with the New Zealand Army team in 1919 because his father was West African.
He later acted as a team selector.
His interest in sport extended to cricket, and he and his brother William both played for Wellington.2,1
He died on 11 August 1953 at age 67.1
