In the census of 7 April 1861 she was listed as the head of household at Wellington Street, Batley, a widow aged 43, a machine maker. Though I believe her husband to be the William who died in 1855, Elizabeth seems to have been able to keep the business going until her son was old enough to take over.4
Both widowed, Elizabeth Hall married Thomas Heaton on 26 August 1863 at Batley Thomas was described as of Huddersfield and a Superintendent of Police.1

George Wrigley

#3403, b. circa 1819
George Wrigley|b. c 1819|p114.htm#i3403|Benjamin Wrigley||p286.htm#i7959||||||||||||||||
     George Wrigley was born circa 1819; place of birth variously given in censuses as Morley, Batley and Bruntcliffe. He was the son of Benjamin Wrigley. George Wrigley appeared as a cloth drawer aged 20 on the census of 6 June 1841 in the household of Joseph Jubb at Spa Hill, Batley.1 George Wrigley married Martha Newsome, daughter of William Newsome and Mary Gledhill, on 26 May 1844 at Batley George's father's name is given as Benjamin.2
He was listed in the census of 30 March 1851 at Havercroft, Batley, as a cloth drawer aged 31.
George Wrigley married Hannah Parker, daughter of Joseph Jubb and Letitia Talbot, in 1857 her first husband Matthew Parker having died ten years earlier.3
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Billy Well Lane, Batley, as a cloth drawer and rag merchant.4
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Wellington St, Batley, as a Cotton Warp manufacturer.5
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Burnley Mill Yard, Batley, Woollen manufacturer employing 230 men and women.6
In 1886 George Wrigley was was elected an Alderman.
He was listed in the census of 5 April 1891 at Hilberoyd Rd, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer, widower.7
He was listed as head of household in the 1901 census at Hilberoyd Rd, Batley. Retired Woollen manufacturer.8

     Dixon Hall was born on 25 September 1844.1 He was the son of William Hall and Harriet Pycock. In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as as a scholar aged 6 the son of William Hall at Clerk Green, Batley.2
He was listed in the census of 7 April 1861 at Keighley as an ironmonger's apprentice.3
Circa 1867 Dixon Hall took over an ironmongery business in Commercial Street, Batley, which had belonged to the recently deceased William Roberts.
He married Pamelia Colbeck, daughter of Isaac Colbeck and Elizabeth Ineson, in 1869 at St Thomas's Church, Batley.4
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Lower Soothill.4
William Hall Watson was apprenticed to his uncle Dixon Hall when he was 15, and was to remain connected with the firm for some 65 years in 1882 (an unknown value).1,5 He died on 14 March 1917 at Park Villa, Grosvenor Rd, Batley, at age 72; aged 73.1
