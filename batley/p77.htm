William Colbeck

#2290, b. 6 February 1783, d. 19 October 1849
William Colbeck|b. 6 Feb 1783\nd. 19 Oct 1849|p77.htm#i2290|Henry Colbeck|b. 1736|p70.htm#i2101|Millicent Nowell|b. 1742|p71.htm#i2126|Samuel Colbeck|b. 1708|p38.htm#i1134|Ann Fearnsides|d. 1737|p70.htm#i2099|||||||
     William Colbeck was born on 6 February 1783.1 He was the son of Henry Colbeck and Millicent Nowell. William Colbeck was baptized on 20 February 1783 at Batley.1
He married Elizabeth Richardson on 27 March 1806 at Drypool.2
William Colbeck The marriage record describes William as a soldier in the 3rd West Yorkshire Militia. In the early years of the century invasion by Napoleon was seen as a threat, though it does not appear that the 3rd West Riding saw any active seervice, but were stationed in various towns around the country.3
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Havercroft, Batley, as a grocer.4
William Colbeck said to have had a coal pit in a field which must have been close to where he lived. He is had to have had a flair for locating coal beds, and to have at one stage managed the Earl of Wilton's colliery. an unknown person colliery.3
He made a will on 18 April 1845 with a codicil added 2 days before his death. His wife was to have the use of 3 rooms in his house, and a weekly payment of 5 shillings, his grandson William Dyson received £20, and the rest was to be sold to provide allownces for his wife and two younger daughters. Executors were George Sheard and Benjamin Senior.3
William Colbeck died on 19 October 1849 at Havercroft, Batley, at age 66 His death certificate tells us that he died of cholera. A John Newsome was in attendance, and William's occupation was given as coal miner.3

Michael Spedding

#2292, b. 28 February 1834, d. 29 September 1927

     Michael Spedding was born on 28 February 1834 at Huddersfield. He was the son of Robert Spedding and Elizabeth Hudson. Michael Spedding married Susan Fox, daughter of Joseph Fox and Elizabeth Farrar, on 28 March 1853 at Batley.1
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Field Hill, Batley, as a cloth finisher.2
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Spring Gardens, Batley, as a confectioner, he has Robert Ainsworth, confectioner's assistant, as a boarder.3
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Field Lane, Batley, as a Bone setter and baker. Has two boarders, Alfred Beckett and John Thomas Rogan, listed as bakers.4
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as the head of household at Whitaker Street, Batley, as a Bone setter.5
In addition to bone-setting Michael advertised a medicament for piles, "Spedding's Easelip."6
Michael Spedding married Eliza Pickles on 10 February 1896 at Dewsbury.7
Michael Spedding died on 29 September 1927 at Batley at age 93.
