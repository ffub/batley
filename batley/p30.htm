Charles James Critchley

#900, b. 10 September 1843, d. 8 October 1890
Charles James Critchley|b. 10 Sep 1843\nd. 8 Oct 1890|p30.htm#i900|James Critchley|b. 1809\nd. 21 Jun 1885|p30.htm#i906|Sarah Jane Illingworth|d. 2 Apr 1886|p30.htm#i907|||||||||||||
     Charles James Critchley was born on 10 September 1843 at Dewsbury.1 He was the son of James Critchley and Sarah Jane Illingworth.
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as as a scholar aged 7 the son of James Critchley at 615 Market Place, Dewsbury.2
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the son of James Critchley at Top of Batley Carr, Batley.3
Charles James Critchley married Ann Sheard, daughter of John Sheard and Sarah Ward, on 7 September 1865 at Batley.4
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as as a woollen manufacturer the son-in-law of John Sheard at Dryfield House, Healey, Batley.5
Charles James Critchley witnessed the marriage of John William Blackburn and Eliza Rebecca Sheard on 23 November 1871 at Batley.4
1881 Charles James Critchley & Co. are listed as woollen manufacturers at Station Road, Batley.6
He married Margaret Talbot, daughter of Joseph Talbot and Ann Walker, on 18 September 1884 at St. Thomas's, Batley, Charles was described as of Highfield House Batley.7
Charles James Critchley died on 8 October 1890 at Westmoreland at age 47.1 He was buried at Batley cemetery. He was residences at Grosvenor Rd(Wilton Park), Batley.

Child of Charles James Critchley and Ann Sheard

  • James Sheard Critchley b. 1866, d. 1866

James Critchley

#906, b. 1809, d. 21 June 1885
     James Critchley was born in 1809 at Warley. He was baptized in 1809 at Sowerby Bridge.
He married Sarah Jane Illingworth on 8 January 1835 at Dewsbury.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Market Place, Dewsbury, as a publican.2
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at 615 Market Place, Dewsbury, as a coal owner and card maker.3
James Critchley was the buyer of Batley Hall in 1857 and rebuilt it in 1860.
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Top of Batley Carr, Batley, as a coal owner employing (9 or 1?) boys and 100 men.4
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Batley Old Hall, Batley, as a coal proprietor.5
He died on 21 June 1885 at Harrogate.6
