Edward Hemingway

#5082, b. 7 May 1800
Edward Hemingway|b. 7 May 1800|p175.htm#i5082|Jacob Hemingway|d. 2 Feb 1847|p179.htm#i5199|Hannah Dean|b. 8 Apr 1771\nd. 9 Jul 1849|p244.htm#i6895|||||||Edward Dean||p244.htm#i6896||||
     Edward Hemingway was born in 1800 at Dewsbury. He was baptized on 7 May 1800 at Dewsbury.1
He was the son of Jacob Hemingway and Hannah Dean. Edward Hemingway married Mary Dennison on 6 April 1836 at Huddersfield The marriage by licence, Edward being described as of Batley and Mary as of Huddersfield. Witnesses were Thomas Dean and Joseph Brooke.2
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Westgate, Dewsbury, as a Blanket Manufacturer.3
Edward Hemingway married Mary Spedding, daughter of Michael Spedding and Hannah Ineson, on 7 November 1841 at Batley Edward is described as of the Aldams Mill Company, and Mary's father as of the Thick(sic) Lane Mill, Batley.
Witnesses were Michael Spedding and Joshua Porritt.4,5
Edward Hemingway married Louisa Wilks in 1849.
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Aldams Head, Town End, Dewsbury, as a blanket manufacturer employing 30 men.6

  • William Hemingway b. 10 Apr 1837
  • Mary Hemingway b. c 1838
  • Thomas Dean Hemingway b. 30 May 1840

Child of Edward Hemingway and Mary Spedding
