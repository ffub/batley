     George Sheard was born on 29 May 1802 at Batley. He was the son of William Sheard and Grace Lister. George Sheard was baptized on 8 August 1802 at Batley.1
He married Rebecca Blakeley, daughter of Samuel Blakeley and Ann Shaw, on 25 April 1822 at Batley witnesses David Sheard and Thomas Law.1
On 18 February 1840 George Sheard with John Sheard and William Sheard were probably the ones brought before the court at Wakefield as insolvent debtors, all three being described as cloth manufacturers. There were other Sheards of the same names, but these look likeliest as brothers.2
George Sheard married Martha Talbot, daughter of John Talbot and Hannah Carr, on 12 October 1840 at All Saints, Wakefield, as Martha Sheard or Talbot.
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at New Batley, Batley, as a clothier, with second wife Martha and her some of her children by first husband, David Sheard. George's own children from his previous marriage are being looked after by grandparents.3
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at N Side Commercial St, Havercroft, Batley, as a hand loom weaver living with son Titus and his own mother Grace, who one imagines, having previously looked after George's older children, has helped with Titus's upbringing. Grace, however, was to die in 1854.4
George Sheard died in 1852 at Batley. He was buried on 29 April 1852 at Batley.1

     Joseph Senior Sheard was born on 3 December 1864 at Batley. He was the son of Benjamin Sheard and Mary Senior. Joseph Senior Sheard was baptized on 13 January 1865 at Batley.
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the son of Benjamin Sheard at Elmwood House, Batley.1
Joseph Senior Sheard was educated at Wakefield Grammar School and Cheltenham School.
He was listed in the census of 1881 at Cheltenham College, Cheltenham, Gloucestershire, with his brother Thomas Clifton Sheard.2
On 18 October 1888 Joseph Senior Sheard married Mary Sunderland Talbot, daughter of Joseph Talbot and Ann Walker, at Batley witnesses were Benjamin Sheard and Joseph Talbot.3
In the census of 31 March 1901 he was listed as the head of household at Elmdene, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer.4
Joseph Senior Sheard Town councillor, elected for North Ward with a record poll
J.P. After the Sheard Firm was sold in 1907 he was associated for many years with Messrs Robert Brearley & Sons, Queen St. Mills. In 1917 Joseph Senior Sheard was a member of the Board of Control for the Woollen Industry, established because of concerns for the availability of wool supplies, and the need for army clothing to have priority.5
He lived in 1922 at Elmdene, Upper Batley. He died on 25 December 1923 at Batley at age 59of a heart attack. He was buried on 28 December 1923 at the Cemetery, Batley.
