Caroline Jackson

#4094, b. circa 1840
     Caroline Jackson was born circa 1840.
Caroline Jackson married David Sheard, son of Benjamin Sheard and Margaret Gledhill, on 20 September 1860 at Batley.1
In the census of 7 April 1861 Caroline Sheard and her husband appeared in the household of her mother-in-law Margaret Sheard at Old Mill Lane, Batley.2
In 1862 Caroline and David Sheard emigrated to America with their daughter, sailing from Liverpool to New York on the "Constitution of Liverpool" and arriving on the 27th August.3
In the census of 1870 Caroline Sheard was listed at Newburgh, Orange, New York, as wife of David Sheard.4
In the census of 8 June 1880 Caroline Sheard was listed at Philadelphia, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, as wife of David Sheard.5

Relationship=3rd cousin 4 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     Mary Stubley was born in 1826 at Batley. She was the daughter of Grace Stubley. Mary Stubley was baptized on 13 August 1826 at Batley.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 se was listed as a woollen rag sorter aged 14 the granddaughter of Betty Stubley.2

     Sarah Stubley was born in 1828. She was the daughter of Noah Stubley. Sarah Stubley was baptized on 8 August 1828 at Batley.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 she was listed as as a female servant the granddaughter of Betty Stubley.2 In the census of 30 March 1851 she was listed as a cloth burler the daughter of Noah Stubley at J Ward's Hill, Batley.3
In the census of 7 April 1861 she was listed as a cloth birler the daughter of Noah Stubley at Fox Row, Batley.4
