John Ward

#1289, b. 15 March 1830, d. 1895
John Ward|b. 15 Mar 1830\nd. 1895|p44.htm#i1289|Charles Ward|b. 12 May 1799\nd. 7 Dec 1869|p7.htm#i203|Ann Birkby|b. 18 Apr 1797\nd. 4 Feb 1837|p7.htm#i204|Samuel Ward|b. 18 Mar 1766\nd. 11 Mar 1822|p7.htm#i206|Hannah Spedding|b. 1773\nd. 20 Aug 1851|p7.htm#i207|John Birkby|b. 2 May 1760|p293.htm#i8151|Sarah Spawforth|b. 1 Mar 1773\nd. 21 Apr 1815|p293.htm#i8152|

Relationship=3rd great-uncle of Vivien Eley.
     John Ward was born on 15 March 1830 at Batley.1 He was the son of Charles Ward and Ann Birkby. John Ward was baptized on 26 April 1830 at Batley.
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the son of Charles Ward at Carlinghow, Batley.2
John Ward married Emma Willans, daughter of William King Willans and Elizabeth Smith, on 10 October 1849 at Batley.3,4

In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Carlinghow, Batley, as a corn miller.5
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Carlinghow, Batley, as a corn miller, master employing 5 men. He was the last of the millers at the Manor corn mill at Carlinghow, where water from the Beck was channeled and dammed to power a water wheel.6 In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Commercial Street, Batley, a butcher. Entry is close to those for Ward's Hill.7
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Blakeridge Lane, Batley, as a butcher.
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as the head of household at Bleak House, Batley, as a retired butcher. Also present is a niece, Susan Walker aged 12.8
John Ward died in 1895. He was buried in 1895 at the cemetery, Batley.
