It would, according to a later account have been in about .4
In 1866 Edwin who worked for Dodgshuns in Germany is likely to have been behind this message presumed to be in search of his brother Jeremiah who was in Australia.
A newspaper account in 1870 tells us that about 13 years earlier Edwin had been in financial difficulties and obliged to compound with his creditors. In fact although legally he had no further obligation, to them, being now in a position to pay them he invited them to the offices of his then employers, Messrs Isaac Dodgshun & Co. of Leeds to receive the remaining part of what he owed them; even offering interest on it, which they declined.4
