     George Stubley was born on 15 March 1780. He was the son of Mark Stubley and Sally Pyrah. George Stubley was baptized on 16 April 1780 at Parish Church, Batley.1
He began military service in July 1799 as a private in the 35th Infantry.2 He received a gun shot wound in the head in action. This must have been in a campaign of which there is a good account on a website about the 35th Foot. 21 April 1807 Egypt.3
He ended military service in November 1818 at Brighton; discharged in consideration of length of service, his conduct described as good. In appearance he was 5'8" tall with brown hair and hazel eyes.4
He married Hannah Lister, daughter of Jacob Lister and Sarah Stubley, on 20 May 1819 at Parish Church, Batley, witnesses Benjamin Sheard and Joshua Stubley.1
On 3 August 1840 George, his first wife having died in February, and now described as a widower of full age, son of Mark Stubley, married Elizabeth Micklethwaite, daughter of Daniel Dyson, at Batley She was married as Elizabeth Micklethwaite, widow, daughter of Daniel Dyson, weaver. Witnesses were Thomas Stogdale and Joshua Porrit.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Havercroft, Batley, as a weaver aged 60.5
George Stubley appeared as a Chelsea Pensioner aged 72 on the census of 30 March 1851 in the household of his daughter and son-in-law Jesse Idal.6
George Stubley died in 1852 at Havercroft, Batley. He was buried on 18 February 1852 at Batley ; a very faint record on fiche, which I think is he, for whom the age is right.1

Ancestry of Vivien Eley Tomlinson
     Thomas Stubley was born in 1703. He was the son of John Stubley and Susanna (?). Thomas Stubley was baptized on 20 February 1702/3 at Birstall as son of John Stubley of Heckmondwike. I was unable to find this entry in fiches, some of which are unreadable, but it is shown in the transcripts, which incorporate information from Bishop's Transcripts.1

Thomas Stubley, described as of Batley, married Sarah Williamson on 28 June 1727 at Dewsbury Thomas is described as of Batley.2
Thomas Stubley was possibly the Thomas accused by a Henry and William Popplewell of Hawksworth of stealing a sheep from their father William in 1741 its carcase, identified by a mark on the fleece, having been found in Thomas's barn. Thomas denied knowledge of how it came to be in the old barn, which was part fallen in and open.
Again we know nothing of the outcome, but I have found no further trace of Thomas around Guiseley. A period spent in that area does, however, correspond with a break in children born to Thomas in Batley. If he escaped being severely penalised, he may still have been obliged to leave the area.
The Popplewell involvement in both cases may be significant. If Thomas and his brother Abraham are who I suppose, then they had a sister Ann who married a William Popplewell in Birstall.3
