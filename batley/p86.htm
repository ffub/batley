William James Richardson Fox

#2597, b. 1833, d. 31 January 1920
William James Richardson Fox|b. 1833\nd. 31 Jan 1920|p86.htm#i2597|David Fox|b. 1808\nd. Relationship=3rd cousin 4 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     William James Richardson Fox was born in 1833. He was the son of David Fox and Rachel Jubb. William James Richardson Fox was baptized on 29 December 1833 at Batley.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the son of David Fox at Spa Hill, Batley.2
William James Richardson Fox was educated; Batley Grammar School and Mr. Wilkinson's Academy.3
William James Richardson Fox was employed in the office of his grandfather, Joseph Jubb in 1848.
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as a clerk to wool firm the son of Rachel Fox at Havercroft, Batley.4
William James Richardson Fox married Emma Riley, daughter of Ely Riley, in 1855 at Parish Church, Elland.
William James Richardson Fox and Joseph Jubb Fox set up in business as Messrs W J R Fox & Co, his brother Josephworking with him for many years. on 31 March 1869 at Perseverance Mills, Batley.
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Hanover St, Batley, as a manufacturer of woollen felts. Employs 19 men, 14 women and 2 boys.
In 1878 William James Richardson Fox was associated with George Blackburn in coming up with the idea of a public meeting which led to the provision of a hospital in Batley.5,6
William James Richardson Fox was Mayor of Batley between 1879 and 1880. He lived at Grosvenor House, Batley.
He died on 31 January 1920 at Batley.

Robert Talbot

#2599, b. 1826, d. 29 March 1907
Robert Talbot|b. 1826\nd. 29 Mar 1907|p86.htm#i2599|Joseph Talbot|b. 20 Jan 1798|p82.htm#i2470|Mary Bennington|b. c 1803|p82.htm#i2474|James Talbot|b. 1759\nd. 5 Mar 1840|p48.htm#i1426|Mary Sunderland|b. c 1761\nd. 17 Jul 1820|p48.htm#i1427|||||||
     Robert Talbot was born in 1826. He was the son of Joseph Talbot and Mary Bennington. Robert Talbot was baptized on 24 September 1826 at Batley.
He married Leah Blakeley, daughter of John Blakeley and Mary Blackburn.1,2
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Field Hill, Batley, as a widower, an overlooker in a woollen mill. This was Colbeck's Cheapside Mill, and Malcolm Haigh tells us that he came to be chief wool buyer and blender there.3
Robert Talbot married Ellen Preston in 1863.
In 1865 Robert Talbot and Joseph Talbot set up in business for themselves, first at Birstall and later at Morley, taking into partnership their cousin Samuel Senior.
Robert Talbot was in business with his brother as R and J Talbot and Co., later joined by their cousin Samuel Senior. In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Stanley Terrace, Batley, as a Town Councillor, Woollen manufacturer, master, employing 110 males, 111 females. farmer of 170 acres, master, employing 2 labourers. Also employs one domestic seervant.4
In 1878 Robert Talbot was associated with George Blackburn in coming up with the idea of a public meeting which led to the provision of a hospital in Batley.5,6
Robert Talbot lived at Alton Lodge, Upper Batley, Batley.
He died on 29 March 1907.7

For many years William Henry Blackburn was, along with his father, involved in the firm of John Blackburn, Old Mill.
From 1876 William Henry Blackburn he took an active part in the local K.O.Y.L.I. volunteers, rising to be commandant of the Batley detachment.

William Henry Blackburn married Annie Wrigley, daughter of George Wrigley and Hannah Jubb, on 16 March 1882 at Batley Witnesses were George Wrigley and George Blackburn.4
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as the head of household at Mostyn Villas, Batley, a Shoddy traveller.5
William Henry Blackburn was now living next to his brother- in- law James Parker.
Circa 1892 William Henry Blackburn left the Old Mill to join a venture with his father George Blackburn at Providence Mill, Birstall, as Messrs George Blackburn & Son.6
In the census of 31 March 1901 he was listed as the head of household at Mostyn Villas, Batley, a Shoddy manufacturer.7
