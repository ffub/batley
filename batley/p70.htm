Joseph Colbeck

#2119, b. 15 February 1787, d. 1 July 1855
Joseph Colbeck|b. 15 Feb 1787\nd. 1 Jul 1855|p70.htm#i2119|Joseph Colbeck|b. 13 Jan 1761\nd. 1833|p70.htm#i2113|Ann Lindley|b. 1761\nd. 1835|p70.htm#i2114|Henry Colbeck|b. 1736|p70.htm#i2101|Betty Eastwood|b. 1737|p70.htm#i2102|Thomas Lindley||p70.htm#i2116||||
     Joseph Colbeck was born on 15 February 1787. He was the son of Joseph Colbeck and Ann Lindley. Joseph Colbeck was baptized on 7 March 1787 at Batley.
Joseph Colbeck was was said to have been a spinner at the Old Mill in his youth.
Joseph was than said to have served 8 years in the army at a time when conscription was in force. A man at Methley is said to have wanted someone to take his place, and Joseph did so for a fee. He did not serve abroad, but somewhere on his travels he met a young lady from Devon who became his first wife.1
He married Harriet Chown on 11 July 1814 at Batley.2
Joseph Colbeck married Sarah Constantine as his second wife on 9 September 1819 at Tong.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Carlinghow, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer.3
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Carlinghow, Batley, as a grocer and cloth weaver.4
Joseph Colbeck died on 1 July 1855 at age 68.5

Children of Joseph Colbeck and Sarah Constantine

  • James Colbeck+ b. 1820, d. 1914
  • David Colbeck b. c 1825
  • Rachel Colbeck b. c 1826
