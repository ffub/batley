     Martha Sheard was born on 23 February 1844. She was the daughter of Isaac Sheard and Harriet Stubley. Martha Sheard was baptized on 7 April 1844 at Batley as daughter of Isaac and Harriet Sheard.1
In the census of 30 March 1851 she was listed as a scholar the daughter of Isaac Sheard at 16 J Ward's Hill, Batley.2
In the census of 7 April 1861 she was listed as a woollen weaver the daughter of Isaac Sheard at Back Street, Mirfield.
Martha Sheard married Josiah Clafton, son of George Clafton and Ann Wilby, on 17 December 1863 at Mirfield.3,4
In the census of 2 April 1871 Martha Clafton was listed at Ravens St, Mirfield, as wife of Josiah Clafton.5

Martha Clafton and Josiah emigrated in 1872 to Little Falls, New York, sailing on the "Spain" arrriving in New York on the 26th August.6,7
Martha Clafton, widowed the previous year, accompanied her aunt Mary Seniorand Isabella Mitchell and Ann Sheard on a visit to England in 1889. The party left New York in June and returned in October, arriving on the 15th on the "Aurania."7
In the census of 1892 she was listed as the head of household at Little Falls, Herkimer, New York, as a mill hand aged 48.8
In the census of 1900 she was listed as the head of household at Little Falls, Herkimer, New York, as a widow.9
In the census of 23 April 1910 she was listed as the head of household at Little Falls, Herkimer, New York.10
In the census of 12 January 1920 she was listed as the head of household at 6 Webster Street, Little Falls, Herkimer, New York.11
Martha Clafton died in 1929 at Little Falls, NY.
She was buried at Wilcox cemetery, Little Falls.

     Jacob Sheard was born on 28 August 1845. He was the son of Isaac Sheard and Harriet Stubley. Jacob Sheard was baptized on 21 September 1845 at Batley.1
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as a scholar the son of Isaac Sheard at 16 J Ward's Hill, Batley.2
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as Isaac, but is the age of Jacob in other censuses, a cloth finisher the son of Isaac Sheard at Back Street, Mirfield.
Jacob Sheard married Ann Naylor, daughter of Joseph Naylor, in 1867.3
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Inman Fold, Ravensthorpe, as a cloth finisher.4
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Albion Street, Mirfield, as a woollen cloth fuller.5
In 1883 Jacob Sheard and Ann Sheard arrived in Little Falls, NY having sailed on the Wisconsin with three children, Isaac, Florence and Alice, arriving in New York on the 19th June.6,7 Travelling with Jacob Sheard and family on the Wisconsin were two relatives Frank Sheard, Ethelbert Ineson and Mary Ineson, Ethelbert's mother.
In the census of 1892 he was listed as the head of household at Little Falls, Herkimer, New York.8
In the census of 1900 he was listed as retired, the head of household at 48½ Furnace St, Little Falls, Herkimer, New York.9
Jacob Sheard died on 12 August 1901 at Utica at age 55.

William Colbeck Parker|b. c 1850|p105.htm#i3138|Edmund Augustus Parker|b. c 1821|p102.htm#i3057|Ann Colbeck|b. c 1810\nd. 26 Mar 1887|p82.htm#i2492|||||||William Colbeck|b. 6 Feb 1783\nd. 19 Oct 1849|p77.htm#i2290|Elizabeth Richardson|b. c 1785\nd. 3 Dec 1856|p106.htm#i3160|
     William Colbeck Parker was born circa 1850. He was the son of Edmund Augustus Parker and Ann Colbeck. In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as aged 1 the son of Edmund Augustus Parker at Havercroft, Batley.1
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as as a scholar the son of Edmund Augustus Parker at Colbeck Row, Batley.2
William Colbeck Parker married Frances Eliza Isherwood Hogg in 1871.3
He was listed in the census of 3 April 1881 at Jubb's Mill Yard, Batley, as a commercial clerk. Given his place of residence it would seem likely that he worked for Jubbs.4
In 1884 William Colbeck Parker was bankrupt, described by then as a manufacturer.5

Children of William Colbeck Parker and Frances Eliza Isherwood Hogg

  • Margaret A Parker b. c 1873
  • Agnes Sheard Parker b. 1877
  • William Parker b. c 1878

George Luff Stocker

#3139, b. circa 1834
     George Luff Stocker was born circa 1834 at Toddington, Bedfordshire. In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at 5 Cloth Fair, London, as a -----broker wool merchant heading a household which includes a brother, John C Stocker, foreman over wool department, a cousin, an aunt and a domestic servant.1
He married Helen Sheard, daughter of James Sheard and Ann Colbeck, on 24 October 1861 at Batley.2,3
In the census of 28 July 1870 he was listed as as a woollen manufacturer, as was his brother John C Stocker, in the large establishment apparently headed by John Bentley at East Windsor, Hartford, Connecticut.4

     Rachael Sheard was born on 26 November 1853 at Batley.1 She was the daughter of Peter Lister Sheard. Rachael Sheard was baptized on 5 February 1854 at Batley.1
In the census of 7 April 1861 she was listed as aged 7 the daughter of Peter Lister Sheard at Newsome's Yard, Batley.2
Rachael Sheard appeared as a cloth weaveron the census of 2 April 1871 in the household of William Beaumont and Grace Beaumont at Mill Lane, Batley; a mill hand.3 In 1873 Rachael Sheard and Hannah Sheard were probably the two girls who went to New York from Liverpool on the "Calabria", arriving there on the first of September. The ages, 19 and 17 are correct, and although Hannah's name looks more like Honora we can't find a possible Honora Sheard. Nor can we find the sisters later in England.4
