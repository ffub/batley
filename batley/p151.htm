Joseph Marriott

#4467, b. circa 1852, d. 3 January 1883
Joseph Marriott|b. c 1852\nd. 3 Jan 1883|p151.htm#i4467|John Thomas Marriott|b. c 1821|p130.htm#i3918|Letitia Jubb|b. 1823|p75.htm#i2245|||||||Joseph Jubb|b. 2 Jul 1784\nd. 24 Jan 1867|p75.htm#i2237|Letitia Talbot|b. 2 Apr 1787\nd. 22 Oct 1831|p49.htm#i1430|

Relationship=4th cousin 3 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     Joseph Marriott was born circa 1852 at Batley. He was the son of John Thomas Marriott and Letitia Jubb. In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as a manufacturer of cloth the son of John Thomas Marriott at Healey, Batley.1
Joseph Marriott had been working for his cousin in at Fischamend, Austria. On 2 January 1883 Joseph Marriott was stabbed several times and and his cousin George Bennet Jubb, was fatally wounded, dying early the following morning. The Danube had burst its banks, and there was heavy flooding. It appears to have been on account of this that female weavers were housed in a barn in poor conditions. The story goes that George and his companions, Joe Marriott and Dick Carter, who had been in an adjacent brewhouse, went to see if they were warm enough, and was ordering wine to be warmed for the women when a young man, perhaps a sweetheart of one of the women, broke in. They tried to get him to leave, but he and his comrades attacked George and Joe.
Joseph Marriott (an unknown value) on 3 January 1883 at Fischamend, Austria.2
