Dock Ing Mill

This mill is said to have been built by Mr David Healey, probably in the late 1860s. When it was offered for sale in 1873, following the death of David Healey the previous year , this was described as comprising 2 warehouses and a dwelling house in the possession or occupation of representatives of David Healey and Benjamin Stubley. It was then bought by Messrs J H Fox & Co. who enlarged it, later passing into the hands of Messrs Schofield Lobley & Co.1

By 1891, when it was destroyed by fire, it was in the hands of Charles Robinson), whose Victoria Mill was to be destroyed by fire some 10 days later. A misprint rather endearingly refers to it as the Little Duckling Mill! 2
