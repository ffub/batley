Arthur Senior

#451, b. 2 May 1873, d. 22 August 1961
Arthur Senior|b. 2 May 1873\nd. 22 Aug 1961|p15.htm#i451|Josephus Senior|b. 1830\nd. 1913|p15.htm#i454|Christiana Hirst|b. 25 Dec 1831\nd. 1889|p15.htm#i455||||Caroline Senior|b. c 1812\nd. 1852|p16.htm#i461|John Hirst|b. c 1792|p16.htm#i462|Mary Hobson|b. 1 Nov 1804|p19.htm#i581|

     Arthur Senior was born on 2 May 1873 at Skelmanthorpe. He was the son of Josephus Senior and Christiana Hirst. Arthur Senior was baptized on 3 August 1873 at St Augustine, Scissett.1
He is said to have been only a baby when the family moved to Batley, and In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as there as a scholar aged 7, the son of Josephus Senior at Suffolk St, Batley.2

He attended Batley Parish Church School, and having passed Standard VII he was appointed a monitor. He served 5 years as pupil-teacher, then became a certificated assistant on 8 August 1887.
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as as a Day School teacher the son of Josephus Senior.3
In the census of 31 March 1901 17 Norfolk St, Batley he was listed as a certificated teacher, the son of Josephus Senior.4

Between 1895 and 1905 Arthur was Assistant Master at the School. My photograph shows him at this time, the seated figure on the right, whilst the seated figure on the left is the then Headmaster, Walter Hurst, a man who did much to transform the school in his long headship from 1881 to 1924, and who would have been responsible for much of my grandfather's training.

Arthur Senior married Sarah Grace Gertrude Binns, daughter of Isaac Binns and Sarah Thurman Robinson, on 7 August 1902 at Purlwell Wesleyan Chapel, Batley, Although both by then working in Batley the couple are said to have met on a holiday in Devon.

Arthur Senior was Headmaster of Cawthorne Boys School between 1905 and 1906 at Cawthorne, Barnsley.5  Then Headmaster of Cawthorne Mixed School between 1906 and 1910.5

Between 1910 and 1915 Arthur Senior was Headmaster of the Council School at Clayton, Bradford.5

When his children were young Arthur Senior rented the Schoolhouse in Conistone, close to where his sister Maria lived, for the summer holidays.

Life in Clayton was all too soon be overshadowed by the outbreak of the first World War. He was one of the founders of the Clayton Volunteer Company, the first to be founded in the Bradford Area, then felt impelled to join up himself. He joined the OTC at Leeds University, and was gazetted Lieutenant in May 1815 at the age of 42. He did not see service abroad, but was promoted to the rank of Captain, and his last responsibility during the war period was to be in charge of the the Guards at British Dyes and other important adjoining works at Huddersfield.6 Arthur Senior resumed his position of Headmaster at Clayton Council School when his military service ended in 1919.7,5

In January 1925 Arthur Senior was appointed Headmaster of his own old school, Batley Parish Church School, and the family lived at the adjacent School House at Stocks Lane, Batley, Note the glimpse of the Churchyard in its former state in the photograph showing him with his staff.5,8
Arthur Senior wrote some of his own exercise books, which were published in 1932 and were used in the Parish Church School. Barbara Hayes kindly gave me her own copies, still carefully backed in brown paper (removed to make a better photo, but now replaced).

Arthur Senior retired in 1937.
Benjamin Ineson was a lifelong friend of my grandfather Arthur Senior and like him became a local headmaster, in his case of Milll Lane School. Speaking on the presentation of a gift to my grandfather following his retirement in 1938 he said:-
"that he had known Captain Senior more years than he cared to remember. He remembered him as a tall lad at school over 50 years ago. He envied Captain Senior then because he had long trousers. Later, as a young man, he spent some hectic evenings, leaving Captain Senior's abode at four or five o'clock in the morning."9

He was to have a long retirement, though sadly interrupted soon by another war, in which he served as Deputy Chief Air Raid Warden for Batley.
Arthur Senior and Sarah had bought a house on Upper Batley Lane, and I remember often staying with them. Grandpa retained the fitness of a younger man, and I would go on long walks with him. Sometimes we went into Batley, past the Bagshaw Museum and through Wilton Park, or into Birstall or round Howley.
He died on 22 August 1961 at age 88. He was buried on 25 August 1961 at the Cemetery, Batley.

Sarah Grace Gertrude Binns appeared as a school teacher on the census of 31 March 1901 living with her stepfather William Todd at 90 Purlwell Lane, Batley, Although his marriage to her mother was of short duration, she had a good relationship with him, and I have a pretty ring he bought her for her 21st, feeling that her own father would have done something of the kind.2

Sarah Grace Gertrude Binns married Arthur Senior, son of Josephus Senior and Christiana Hirst, on 7 August 1902 at Purlwell Wesleyan Chapel, Batley, Although both by then working in Batley the couple are said to have met on a holiday in Devon.

Sarah Grace Gertrude Binns was said to have worked for Women's Emancipation as a young woman, and she continued to take an active interest in politics, several times chairing the women's section of the Batley and Morley Conservative Association and serving on various Yorkshire committees.
She founded the women's section of the local branch of the British Legion.
She witnessed the marriage of Brian Eley to her daughter Grace Senior on 9 September 1933 at Batley Parish Church.3

During the Second World War she was in charge of the Red Cross Depot in Batley, in which capacity she twice received the Princess Royal when she inspected the work being done by the Red Cross and St. John's war organisation. Starting with bandages and items for hospital use, the group went on to make garments and for evacuees, prisoners of war, and troops at the front. In the first year alone more than 4000 garments were produced. Sarah was herself an excellent needlewoman, and and also skilled in the making of bobbin lace.

Sarah Grace Gertrude Senior died on 21 May 1956 at age 78. She was buried on 22 May 1956 at The cemetery, Batley, ; in Grave G 108.

     William Hirst Senior was born in 1904 at Batley. He was the son of Arthur Senior and Sarah Grace Gertrude Binns.
When he was a child for the summer holidays his father Arthur Senior rented the Schoolhouse in Conistone, close to where his sister Maria lived. It was there that William's interest in farming developed through spending time helping his uncle Thomas Hardacre.
He was educated at Bradford Grammar School. William Hirst Senior shone in the Classics stream there, and his father was at first concerned when he wished to read agriculture at University. finding it hard to envisage where this would lead since his parents could not afford to set him up on a farm.1
He did read Agriculture at Reading, following his first degree with an MSc. and a period as a Research Scholar.
In 1929 William joined the Department of Agriculture for Scotland, where he remained, retiring as Under-Secretary in 1966.
He married Olive Kathleen Killick in 1930 and the couple were to have five children.
Between 1941 and 1944 William Hirst Senior was Secretary of the Balfour of Burleigh Committee on Hill Sheep Farming in Scotland. In 1947 William Hirst Senior was made a Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh.
Between 1959 and 1966 William Hirst Senior was a member of the Agricultural Research Council.
Between 1960 and 1966 William Hirst Senior was Chairman of the Scottish Agricultural Improvement Council.
In 1964 William Hirst Senior was awarded the CB.
He died on 11 October 1984 at Innerwick, Dunbar, Scotland.
