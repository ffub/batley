Jeremiah Hudswell

#6550, b. 1830, d. 3 August 1916

Descendants of Joseph Hall
© image courtesy of Steve Wakely
     Jeremiah Hudswell was born in 1830. He was the son of Jeremiah Hudswell and Elizabeth Pyrah. Jeremiah Hudswell was baptized on 15 August 1830 at Batley.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the son of Jeremiah Hudswell at Up Lane, Batley.2
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed at Leeds Road, Huddersfield, lodging with a Sarah Hardy and family, and described as a chemist. (On the same page of the Census is a William Marriot, born in Mirfield, described as a manufacturing chemist employing 10 men and 4 females, and me might speculate as to whether Jeremiah was working for him.).3
In Australia Jeremiah went by the name of Harry.
By the 1860s Jeremiah Hudswell was living at Landsborough, Victoria, Australia, a small goldmining town, part of the goldrush area from the mid 1850s. He does not seem to have made his fortune, but set up in business as a carpenter, painter and undertaker. He chose to use the name of Harry, but probate documents confirm that this was Jeremiah.4 We do not know whether Jeremiah ever saw this notice which his descendant found in an Argus newspaper, but that the message comes through the agency of Dodgshuns suggests that his brother Edwin who worked for them, and whose own financial circumstances were improving, had been instrumental in posting it.
Jeremiah Hudswell married Hannah Newman on 4 July 1868 at Ararat, Victoria, Australia.4

Jeremiah Hudswell died on 3 August 1916 at Landsborough, Victoria, Australia.4 He was buried on 5 August 1916 at Landsborough, Victoria, Australia.4
