Relationship=9th great-grandfather of Vivien Eley.
Charts
Ancestry of Vivien Eley Tomlinson
     Richard Shepley was born in 1591 at Mirfield. He was the son of Thomas Shepley and Jane Hepworth. Richard Shepley was baptized on 26 January 1590/91 at Mirfield.1
He married Ester Rhodes, daughter of William Rhodes and Agnes Hall, on 23 May 1619 at Mirfield.1

Richard Shepley made a will on 18 October 1631; in which he describes himself as a tanner. A servant of Richard Shepley's was buried on 4 November 1631.1 He died in 1631 at Mirfield of the plague, along with "his littell infant" They seem to have been the last of 130 victims of this outbreak of plague, which had also claimed his parents-in-law, said to have been brought to Mirfield by a poor stranger who died there in April of that year.1 He was buried on 19 November 1631 at Mirfield.1

Relationship=8th great-uncle of Vivien Eley.
     Joshua Shepley was born circa 1623 at Mirfield. He was the son of Richard Shepley and Ester Rhodes. Joshua Shepley was baptized on 18 January 1622/23 at Mirfield.1
I think it likely that Joshua Shepley married Margaret Burgh on 4 July 1655 at St Peter's, Leeds, We know from Joshua's grave that his wife was Margaret, and the date fits well.2
Joshua Shepley was listed as having 2 hearths in the Hearth Tax Bill of 1666 at Batley.3
He and Jeremiah Shepley were listed as freeholders in 1677 at Batley.
Joshua Shepley left a will on 16 April 1679 bequests including 20s to his mother, 3s to his brother Jeremy and 3s apiece to Marmaduke and Jeremy his brother Jeremy's sons.
He died in 1679 at Batley.4 He was buried on 21 May 1679 at Batley.5

Henry Wildsmith

#2911, b. circa 1827, d. 25 August 1870
     Henry Wildsmith was brother-in-law of Joshua Carter. He was born circa 1827 at Mirfield. He married Nancy Carter, daughter of Joshua Carter and Ann (?), on 27 August 1848.1

He was listed in the census of 30 March 1851 at Up Lane, Batley, shop keeper of clothes.2

On 10 May 1860 Henry Wildsmith and Joshua Carter together with Joshua's son James Joshua were granted a patent:-

"for improvements in extracting wool or other animal fibres from combinations of wool and cotton or mixtures of other animal and vegetable fibrous substances or fabrics, and in the machinery or apparatus employed therein"
This was a form of the process known as carbonising.3

He was listed in the census of 7 April 1861 at Victoria St, Batley, Contractor and Shoddy Manufacturer.4
Henry Wildsmith died on 25 August 1870 at Carr House, Birstall; aged 44 years.5

Children of Henry Wildsmith and Nancy Carter

  • Louisa Ann Wildsmith b. c 1850
  • Mary Ellen Wildsmith b. 5 Mar 1852
