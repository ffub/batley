Descendants of Michael Sheard born 1688
     David Wilson was born in 1818. He was the son of Robert Wilson and Hannah Sheard. David Wilson was baptized on 27 December 1818 at Batley.1
He married Eunice Wood Illingworth, daughter of John Illingworth, on 6 February 1837 at Dewsbury.2,3
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Havercroft, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer aged 20.4
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as the head of household at Providence Street, Batley, as in: a firm of 5 one of them. Woollen manufacturer employing 35 persons, 18 men 12 women, 5 girls.5
David Wilson married Mary (?).
In 1860 David Wilson was described as manufacturer of Providence Mill, Batley at the time of his son Joseph's wedding.
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household as a woollen cloth manufacturer employing 107 persons. Also present are Mary A Sisson, niece, and Sarah E Gibson, general servant.6
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Taylor Street, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer employing 60 hands.7
On 18 May 1875 David Wilson laid the foundation stone of the new Wesleyan Sunday School adjacent to Hick Lane Chapel.8
He was buried on 15 January 1880 at Batley.9
