John Gott

John Gott|b. 1791\nd. 1867|p298.htm#i8263|Benjamin Gott|b. 1762\nd. 1840|p298.htm#i8264||||||||||||||||
     John Gott was born in 1791. He was the son of Benjamin Gott. John Gott chaired the committeee for the proposed Leeds, Dewsbury and Manchester junction railway, on which George Sheard and Abraham Burnley represented Batley.1 John Gott died in 1867.

Citations

  1. [S253] The Leeds Mercury, "13th July 1844."

Benjamin Gott

#8264, b. 1762, d. 1840
     Benjamin Gott was born in 1762. Had been apprenticed to merchants Wormald and Fountaine in 1780 and was subsequently made a partner in the firm. In 1892 he was the builder of Bean Ing Mill in Leeds, where all the processes of manufacture, though some still carried out by hand , were brought onto the same premises. with loomshops and finishing facilities. He died in 1840.

Squire Senior

#8279, b. 1881, d. March 1938

Relationship=3rd cousin 3 times removed of Vivien Eley.
Charts
Descendants of Joseph Hall
     Squire Senior was born in 1881. He was the son of John Hall Senior and Elizabeth (?). In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as aged 1 week the son of John Hall Senior at Purlwell Lane, Batley.1
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as a scholar the son of John Hall Senior at 34 Purlwell Lane, Batley.2
In 1909Squire Senior emigrated to America, sailing on the SS Campania from Liverpool and arriving in New York on the 29th May. He gave his next of kin's name as his brother Frank Senior of 38 Purlwell Lane, Batley. He gave his destination as Fort Plain, where his uncle by marriage, Chales Bailey, long resident in Little Falls, now had a business.3 He married Adelaide (?).
In 1920 Squire and Adelaide (?) moved to Sacramento, California where Adelaide was said to have relatives.4
Squire Senior died in March 1938 at Los Angeles.5
