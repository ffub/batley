Robert Wooler

#4640, b. 11 April 1770, d. 20 April 1838
     Robert Wooler was born on 11 April 1770. He married Sarah Maud Upton on 27 July 1791 at St. Peter's, Leeds.1,2
In 1823 Robert Wooler gave land on which a first Methodist New Connexion Chapel was erected at the top of Spa Hil in Batley, a building described as "rigidly plain!3 He died on 20 April 1838 at age 68.1

     Margaret Wooler was born on 10 June 1792.1 She was the daughter of Robert Wooler and Sarah Maud Upton. Margaret Wooler was baptized on 11 July 1792 at Dewsbury.2
She was headmistress of Roe Head School in Mirfield, attended by Charlotte Bronte, and where Charlotte made friends with Mary Taylor and Ellen Nussey.

In the census of 2 April 1871 she was listed as aged 78 the sister-in-law of Rev Edward Nicholl Carter at Strawberrie Square, Heckmondwike.

     James Upton Wooler was born on 22 February 1811.1 He was the son of Robert Wooler and Sarah Maud Upton. James Upton Wooler was baptized on 3 April 1811 at Dewsbury.2

James Upton Wooler died on 23 March 1892 at age 81.
