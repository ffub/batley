Henry Sheard

#2982, b. 1827, d. 24 December 1905
Henry Sheard|b. 1827\nd. 24 Dec 1905|p99.htm#i2982|David Sheard|b. 5 Sep 1800\nd. 3 Jan 1838|p3.htm#i87|Martha Talbot|b. 1 Sep 1803\nd. 1842|p48.htm#i1402|Michael Sheard|b. c 1776\nd. 4 Feb 1837|p3.htm#i84|Sarah Lister|b. 1778\nd. 28 Mar 1840|p3.htm#i85|John Talbot|b. 1753\nd. 1 May 1808|p48.htm#i1422|Hannah Carr|b. 9 Apr 1758\nd. 2 Aug 1840|p47.htm#i1387|

Relationship=1st cousin 4 times removed of Vivien Eley.
Charts
Descendants of Michael Sheard born 1688
     Henry Sheard was born in 1827. He was the son of David Sheard and Martha Talbot.
Henry Sheard was baptized on 4 March 1827 at Batley.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was living with John Cowling and his wife Sarah who was his late mother's elder sister.2
Henry Sheard married Ellen Hepworth, daughter of Joseph Hepworth and Ellen Blamires, on 27 February 1851 at Batley witnesses were Joseph Hepworth and Jane Sheard.3,1
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as as a bookkeeper at woollen manufactory the son-in-law of Ellen Hepworth.4 In 1855 Henry Sheard received a £50 legacy from his uncle George Sheard, to be adjusted if Henry had any outstanding debt to him.

He and Samuel Sheard were for a time in partnership as manufacturers with Samuel Senior at Victoria Mill, but he withdrew from the partnership on on 30 December 1857.5 In 1857 Henry Sheard was listed as a manufacturer together with his brother Samuel (Sheard and Senior)The firm are listed as at Victoria Mill under the heading of Blanket Manufacturers and again under the heading of Woollen Manufacturers.6
He and Samuel Sheard with Samuel Seniorwere also among some dozen partners in Toad Pipes Mill, another company venture, where cloth finishing appears to have been carried out, for in 1857 finishing machinery was offered for sale. Soon after that the mill was sold. In 1859 Henry Sheard was living at the bottom of Batley Field Hill in the Poll Book of that year at Batley.
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Field Hill, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer.7
The partnership between brothers Henry Sheard and Samuel Sheard in the trade of woollen manufacturers, trading as Sheards and Senior or under any other name was dissolved on on 10 June 1861 Stock in hand was offered for sale, and consisted of plain and figured pilots, reversibles, coatings, etc.5,8
Henry Sheard and Abraham Talbot were said to have been two of the partners in the Alexandra Mill Company.9
In 1870 Henry Sheard was listed as a woollen manufacturer, Bradford Road and Alexandra Mill.10
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at Batley Field Hill, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer.11
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Field Hill Gothic Cottage, Batley, as a woollen manufacturer.12
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as the head of household at 7 Field Hill, Batley, as an accountant.13
In the census of 31 March 1901 he was listed as the head of household at 7 Field Hill, Batley, as an accountant.14
He died on 24 December 1905.15
