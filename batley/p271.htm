     Joseph Bolton was born circa 1826. He was the son of Sarah Bolton.
Joseph Bolton was baptized on 14 January 1827 at Batley.1
He appeared aged 14 on the census of 6 June 1841 in the household of Richard Bolton at Howley, Morley.2
In the census of 30 March 1851 he was listed as a dyer, the nephew of Elizabeth Bolton.3
Joseph Bolton married Lucy Firth, daughter of John Firth, on 21 December 1851 at Wakefield.
Witnesses were Mark Andrews and P Heptinstall. Joseph gives his father's name as Charles, actually the name of his grandfather.4,5
In the census of 7 April 1861 he was listed as the head of household at Up Lane, Batley, as a fire man.6
In the census of 2 April 1871 he was listed as the head of household at 5 Ilberoyd Road, Batley, as an engineer.7
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as the head of household at Mill lane, Batley, as an engine driver at a woollen factory.8
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as the head of household at 2 Mill Lane, Batley, as an engineer.9
Joseph Bolton died on 20 November 1895 at Leeds; death was ascribed to chronic bronchitis, cardiac failure and asthenia. Son John Firth Bolton was present at his death.4,10

Joseph Bolton

#7589, b. 29 April 1871, d. 22 February 1941

Joseph Bolton. © image courtesy of Ian Bolton
     Joseph Bolton was born on 29 April 1871.1 He was the son of Joseph Bolton and Lucy Firth.
In the census of 3 April 1881 he was listed as a scholar the son of Joseph Bolton at Mill lane, Batley.2
In the census of 5 April 1891 he was listed as a fireman the son of Joseph Bolton at 2 Mill Lane, Batley.3

Joseph Bolton married Emma Newsome, daughter of Joseph Newsome and Martha Preston, on 2 March 1895 at St John the Evangelist, Carlinghow, Batley.1
In the census of 31 March 1901 he was listed as the head of household at 9 Weston View, Holbeck, Leeds, as an engine tenter.
In the census of 1911 he was listed as the head of household at 55 Taylor Street, Batley, as a mechanic, labourer.1

In 1912 Joseph Bolton and Emma Bolton emigrated to Australia sailing on the SS Limerick.1
Arriving in Cairns where Joseph's brother Dan Firth Bolton had been living since 1887, Joseph and family then headed north to the far northern Queensland town of Mount Mulligan wherre he maintained stationery steam engines and could use his coal industry knowledge gained in Yorkshire.1
Joseph Bolton was able to produce good references including one from Soothill Wood Colliery.
By the mid 1920s Joseph and Emma moved back to Cairns and settled into a new cottage at 200 Lake Street recently built for them by their son Joseph Newsome Bolton.1
Joseph Bolton then found work as a stevedore on the Cairns wharf where he remained until his retirement.
He died on 22 February 1941 at the District Hospital, Cairns, at age 69 of carcinoma of the stomach.1
Joseph and Emma were to live to see 15 Australian born grandchildren, but Yorkshire roots were not forgotten when relatives added this plaque to their unmarked grave.
