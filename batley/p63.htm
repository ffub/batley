Joseph Pyrah

#1908, b. 25 April 1806
Joseph Pyrah|b. 25 Apr 1806|p63.htm#i1908|Jeremiah Pyrah|b. 1785\nd. 26 Sep 1810|p63.htm#i1904|Mary Thompson|b. 23 Mar 1785|p6.htm#i164|Joseph Pyrah|b. 1756\nd. 25 Apr 1806|p48.htm#i1429|Elizabeth Gledhill|b. 1760\nd. 5 Oct 1825|p57.htm#i1669|Thomas Thompson|b. 1758\nd. 1840|p6.htm#i160|Ann Chadwick|b. 27 Aug 1760|p6.htm#i161|

Relationship=1st cousin 4 times removed of Vivien Eley.
     Joseph Pyrah was born on 25 April 1806 at Batley. He was the son of Jeremiah Pyrah and Mary Thompson. Joseph Pyrah was baptized on 25 May 1806 at Batley.
He married Jenet Senior, daughter of George Senior and Mary (?), on 6 October 1826 at Batley by licence, witnesses William Kaye and Michael Ward.1
In the census of 6 June 1841 he was listed as the head of household at Staincliffe, Batley, as a Rag merchant.
In the 30 March 1851 census he was listed as the head of household at Staincliffe, Batley, as a rag and flock dealer.2
Joseph Pyrah was listed as a rag merchant in 1853 at Staincliffe, Batley.3

I believe he was the Pyrah instrumental in setting up a mill in Berlin
As to the Pyrah involved, Willans has the name as Josh Pyrah and I spent some time looking through the Pyrahs to see who this might be, eventually coming up with a likely candidate. A Joseph Pyrah was listed in censuses as in the rag trade at Staincliffe, but his stepfather Thomas Hepworth and brother Jeremiah Pyrah were stonemasons so he may well have had some knowledge of building trades. What made this seem the likelier was that he was John Blackburn's cousin, and could well have drawn him into the enterprise.4,5
Joseph Pyrah was probably the J Pyrah listed in a Berlin Directory in 1855 at Köpenicker Strasse 30, Berlin,
(Also listed is Wharton, Minego(Mungo?) u Shoddy fabrik: F. Wharton & Co. and Wharton was another name mentioned by Willans in connection with a Berlin venture).6
John Blackburn was not yet listed.6
In 1856 Joseph Pyrah and John Blackburn were both listed at at Köpenickerstrasse as is F Wharton, "inhaber einer Minego und Shoddyfabrik."6

Ann Mayman|b. 8 Nov 1818|p63.htm#i1914|Thomas Mayman||p63.htm#i1915|Ann Hirst||p64.htm#i1916|||||||||||||
     Ann Mayman was baptized on 8 November 1818 at Dewsbury.
She was the daughter of Thomas Mayman and Ann Hirst. Ann Mayman married Jeremiah Pyrah, son of Jeremiah Pyrah and Mary Thompson, on 13 November 1832 at Wakefield.
In the census of 6 June 1841 Ann Pyrah was listed at Birstall as wife of Jeremiah Pyrah.1
In the census of 30 March 1851 Ann Pyrah was listed at 98 New Wakefield, Dewsbury, as wife of Jeremiah Pyrah.2
Ann Pyrah was listed on the census of 7 April 1861 at Dale St, Batley Carr, as a dressmaker, wife of Jeremiah Pyrah.3

Children of Ann Mayman and Jeremiah Pyrah

  • Joseph Pyrah b. 3 Jan 1833
  • Mary Ann Pyrah b. 17 Feb 1837
  • Jane Pyrah b. Feb 1840
  • Ellen Pyrah b. 21 Apr 1842
  • William Jeremiah Pyrah b. 6 Feb 1846, d. 1847
  • Sarah Pyrah b. 30 Sep 1848
  • Eliza Pyrah b. c 1850
  • Elizabeth Ann Pyrah b. 23 Jun 1851
